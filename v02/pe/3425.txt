KAUSALYĀ V¦. The queen of Janaka, the King of
Mithilā. Once King Janaka decided to sell his palace
and everything he possessed and to go abegging for alms.
But his brave wife Kausalyā convinced her husband by
her reasonable arguments that he should not venture to
do so. Thus she prevented her husband from carrying
out his decision.

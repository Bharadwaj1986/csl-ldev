SUMANTRA¦. A minister of king Daśaratha of Ayodhyā.
<div n="lb"/>The king had eight ministers called Jayanta, Dhṛṣṭi,
[Page763-b+ 64]
<div n="lb"/>Vijaya, Siddhārtha. Arthasādhaka, Aśoka, Mantrapāla
<div n="lb"/>and Sumantra and two priests called Vasiṣṭha and
<div n="lb"/>Vāmadeva. (Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa, Bālakāṇḍa, Canto 7).
<div n="lb"/>Sumantra was the right-hand-man of Dasaratha in all
<div n="lb"/>his activities. It was Sumantra who brought down to
<div n="lb"/>the palace sage Ṛṣyaśṛṅga for the yajña conducted by
<div n="lb"/>the king to have issues. According to chapter 12,
<div n="lb"/>Virāṭa Parva of Mahābhārata (Southern Text) Suman-
<div n="lb"/>tra was Daśaratha's charioteer as well.

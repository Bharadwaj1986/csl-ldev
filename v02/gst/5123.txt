{#अपारपार#}¦ Tatpur. I. n. ({#-रम्#}) (In the Sāṅkhya philosophy.)
<div n="lb">The reverse of {#पारपार#} (see the remark s. v. {#अपार#} I. 2.),
<div n="lb">a technical term to denote the non-acquiescence or non-in-
<div n="lb">difference through not reflecting that since a sensual object
<div n="lb">perishes in consequence of enjoyment, pain or trouble arises
<div n="lb">from its cessation; it is amongst the seventeen {#बुद्धिबध#} (q. v.)
<div n="lb">or obstructions of intellect one of the nine which are the
<div n="lb">converse of the {#तुष्टि#} or acquiescences. According to others the
<div n="lb">term would be {#अनपार#} or {#असुनेत्र#}. E. {#अ#} neg. and {#पारपार#}.
<div n="P">II. m. ({#-रः#}) The farthest bound of that which is illimi-
<div n="lb">table, an epithet of Viṣṇu; e. g. {#पारं परं विष्णुरपारपारः#}
<div n="lb">{#परं परेभ्यः परमार्थरूपी । स ब्रह्मपारः परपारभूतः परः परा-#}
<div n="lb">{#णामपि पारपारः ॥#}. (See the explanation of this verse by
<div n="lb">{%Wilson%} in his {%Viṣṇup.%} p. 113, note 3.) E. {#अपार#} II. and {#पार#}.

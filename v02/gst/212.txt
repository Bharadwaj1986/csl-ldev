{#अक्षर#}¦ Tatpur. I. m. f. n. ({#-रः-रा-रम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Imperishable, in-
decayable. <sup>2</sup> Inalterable.
<div n="P">II. m. ({#-रः#}) <sup>1</sup> A name of Śiva. <sup>2</sup> A name of Viṣṇu.
<sup>3</sup> A sword.
<div n="P">III. n. ({#-रम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Brahma, the supreme being (in this sense
it is said to be also mascul.). <sup>2</sup> Eternal beatitude or ex-
emption from further transmigration. <sup>3</sup> Penance, religious
austerity. <sup>4</sup> Moral merit, law. <sup>5</sup> Sacrifice. <sup>6</sup> A letter of the
alphabet. <sup>7</sup> A syllable. <sup>8</sup> The syllable om. <sup>9</sup> A vowel. <sup>10</sup> A
plant (Achyranthes aspera). <sup>11</sup> The sky or atmosphere.
<sup>12</sup> Water (see {#अक्षित#}). E. {#अ#} neg. and {#क्षर#}. (The gramm.
author. derive this word from {#अश् (अशू),#} uṇ. aff. {#सर#} or
{#क्सरन्#}).

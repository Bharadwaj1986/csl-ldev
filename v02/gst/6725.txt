{#अभ्यस्तमित#}¦ Tatpur. m. f. n. ({#-तः-ता-तम्#}) One towards whom
<div n="lb">the sun has set (scil. while he is asleep), asleep at sunset;
<div n="lb">e. g. {%Gotama:%} {#सूर्याभ्युदितो ब्रह्मचारी तिष्ठेदहरभुञ्जनोऽभ्य-#}
<div n="lb">{#स्तमितश्च रात्रिं जपन्सावित्रीम्#} (where the ellipsis ‘when
<div n="lb">asleep’ follows from the word {#स्वप्न#} in the preceeding pas-
<div n="lb">sage: {#रेतस्कन्दने भये रोगे स्वप्नेऽग्नीन्धनभैक्षचरणानि सप्त-#}
<div n="lb">{#रात्रं कृत्वाज्यहोमः साभिसन्धेर्वा रेतस्याभ्याम्#}).--This is the
<div n="lb">reading acc. to both Calc. edd. of {%Kullūka%} to {%Manu%} 2. 220;
<div n="lb">the Calc. ed. of {%Gotama'%}s {%Sanh.%} (made by {%Bhavanīcharana-%}
<div n="lb">{%vandya,%} as he says, {#बहुप्रयत्नतः#}) has, however, {#सूर्याभ्युदिते#}
<div n="lb">{#…(अ)भ्यस्तमिते च…,#} when it would be necessary to
<div n="lb">take {#सू°#} as equivalent to {#सूर्याभ्युदय#} and {#अभ्य°#} as expressing
<div n="lb">the sense of {#अभ्यस्तमय,#} both words being then locatives
<div n="lb">of a neuter {#°तम्#}. But {%Kullūka'%}s reading seems better as
<div n="lb">being countenanced by analogous expressions in {%Manu%} 2. 221.
<div n="lb">Comp. {#अभिनिर्मुक्त#} and {#अभ्युदित#}. E. {#अभि#} and {#अस्तमित#} (not
<div n="lb">{#अभ्यस्तम्#} and {#इत,#} for {#अभि#} refers to the person or act con-
<div n="lb">cerned by the sunset; similarly in ved. passages which
<div n="lb">contain the combination {#अभ्यस्तमगात्#} or {#अभ्यस्तमियात्,#} the
<div n="lb">rad. {#गा#} or {#इ#} are combined first with {#अस्तम्#} and then with
<div n="lb">{#अभि,#} not with {#अभ्यस्तम्#}); see also the preceding.

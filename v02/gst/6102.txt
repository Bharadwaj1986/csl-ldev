{#अभिनीत#}¦ Tatpur. m. f. n. ({#-तः-ता-तम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Brought near or
towards; e. g. (fig., to the understanding, intelligible) in a
quotation of {%Jayam.%} on the {%Bhaṭṭik.:%} {#भाविकत्वमिति प्राहुः#}
{#प्रबन्धविषयं गुणम् । प्रत्यक्षा इव दृश्यन्ते यत्रार्था भूतभाविनः ।#}
{#चित्रोदात्ताद्भुतार्थत्वं कथाया स्वभिनीतता#} ({%its easy under-%}
{%standing%}) {# । शब्दानुकुलता चेति तस्य हेतुं प्रचक्षते#}. <sup>2</sup> Performed
(as a drama). <sup>3</sup> Fit, proper, suitable; e. g. {%Mah. Śāntip.:%}
{#अस्मिन्नेव प्रकरणे धनंजयमुदारधीः । अभिनीततरं वाक्यमि-#}
{#त्युवाच युधिष्ठिरः#}. <sup>4</sup> Highly ornamented or finished. <sup>5</sup> Even-
minded, placid, patient. <sup>6</sup> Impatient. [The two latter
meanings arise from the different manner in which the
verse of the {%Amarak.:%} {#युक्तेऽतिसंस्कृते मर्षिण्यभिनीतो#} &c.
may be read, viz. either {#°ते मर्षिणि#} or {#तेऽमर्षिणि;#} hence
{%Bharata, Subhūti, Ramānātha%} &c. = {#अक्रोधन#} (or {#अक्रोध#})
or = {#अक्षमावत्;#} {%Bhanūd.,%} however, only = {#क्षन्तृ#} (i. e.
[Page250-b+ 56]
{#मर्षिन्#}); other Koshas have only the meaning ‘impatient’;
e. g. {%Medinī, Śabdaratnāv.:%} = {#अमर्षिन्,#} {%Ajayapāla:%} = {#अ-#}
{#मर्षवत्,#} probably also {%Jaṭādhara:%} {#अभिनीतस्त्रिषु न्याय्ये#}
{#संस्कृतेऽमर्षवत्यपि;#} and {%Hemachandra'%}s {#अभिनीतस्तु न्याय्ये म-#}
{#र्षिणि संस्कृते#} is probably also to be read {#न्याय्येऽम°,#}
for the {%Viśvaprak.%} explains: {#अभिनीतो भवेन्न्याय्ये संस्कृता-#}
{#मर्षिणोरपि#}. The meaning ‘placid’ seems more congenial
with the other meanings of the word; and the meaning
‘impatient’ refers perhaps to an E. {#अ#} neg. and {#भिनीत#}--
compare {#विनीत#} and the Pref. on the particle {#अभि#}--.]

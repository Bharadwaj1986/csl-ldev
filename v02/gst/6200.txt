{#अभिमन्तोस्#}¦ Tatpur. ind. (ved.) (Probably.) To hurt, to in-
jure, to destroy, (comp. {%Nīlak.%} s. v. {#मन्#} with {#अभि#}); e. g.
{%Śatapath.:%} {#अथ हैक उल्मुक्येन दहन्ति । तदु तथा न कुर्यादेष#}
{#वै रुद्रियोऽग्निः स हैनमीश्वरः सपुत्रं सपशुमभिमन्तोः#} ‘for it
is capable to injure (or to destroy) him with his sons and
cattle’; (for the construction of {#ईश्वर#} comp. {%Pāṇ.%} III. 4. 13.).
E. {#मन्#} with {#अभि,#} kṛt aff. {#तोसुन्#} (i. e. the genitive of
an obsolete base {#अभिमन्तु,#} in the sense of the infinitive,
which in the classical dialect would be {#अभिमन्तुम्;#} but with
the udātta on the second syllable, which accent the word
would not have, if it were the sixth case of a regular word
{#अभिमन्तु;#} comp. {%Pāṇ.%} VI. 2. 50.).

{#अपवर्ग#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-र्गः#}) <sup>1</sup> Completion, complete performance
<div n="lb">of an act (comp. {#अपवृक्ति#}); e. g. {#क्रियापवर्गेष्वनुजीविसात्कृ-#}
<div n="lb">{#ताः कृतज्ञतामस्य#} (scil. {#राज्ञः#}) {#वदन्ति संपदः;#} in this sense
<div n="lb">the word is used in the {%Mīmānsā Sūtras,%} e. g. {#क्रियाणामर्थ-#}
<div n="lb">{#शेषत्वात्प्रत्यक्षतस्तन्निर्वृत्त्यापवर्गः स्यात् । धर्ममात्रे त्वदर्शनाच्छ-#}
<div n="lb">{#ब्दार्थेनापवर्गः स्यात्;#} and in {%Pāṇini'%}s {%Sūtr.%} e. g. {#अपवर्गे तृ-#}
<div n="lb">{#तीया;#} or in the {%Vārtt.%} to III. 2. 123: {#न्याय्या त्वारम्भानप-#}
<div n="lb">{#वर्गात्#} (see {#अपवृक्त#}). Comp. also {#क्रियापवर्ग#} in distinction
<div n="lb">from {#साधनापवर्ग#}. <sup>2</sup> Complete delivery, i. e. the delivery
<div n="lb">of the soul from the body and consequent exemption from
<div n="lb">the sufferings connected with transmigration; final beatitude
<div n="lb">(Snonymous terms are {#मुक्ति, मोक्ष, निःश्रेयस;#} but while
<div n="lb">{#अपवर्ग#} is used in the sense ‘final beatitude’ especially in the
<div n="lb">{%Sāṅkhya-, Yoga-%} and {%Nyāya Sūtras,%} the former terms (qq. vv.)
<div n="lb">have been generally preferred, to express the same notion,
<div n="lb">by the authors of the {%Vedānta%} and {%Vaiśeṣika S.;%} for the latter
<div n="lb">and the {%Mīmānsā S.%} compare also the preceding and the
<div n="lb">following meaning.); e. g. {%Nyāya Sūtr.:%} {#तदत्यन्तविमोक्षो#}
<div n="lb">{#ऽपवर्गः#} ‘final beatitude is the complete deliverance from it
<div n="lb">(i. e. from pain)’; {%Sāṅkhya Sūtr.:%} {#द्वयोरेकतरस्य वौदासीन्यम-#}
<div n="lb">{#पवर्गः#} ‘final beatitude is the indifference of both or of either
<div n="lb">of them (viz. of Matter and Soul, i. e. their separation);
[Page176-a+ 56]
<div n="lb">{%Yoga Sūtr.:%} {#प्रकाशक्रियास्थितिशीलं भूतेन्द्रियात्मकं भोगाप-#}
<div n="lb">{#वर्गार्थं दृश्यम्#} ({%Bhojadeva:%} {#अपवर्गो विवेकख्यातिपूर्विका संसा-#}
<div n="lb">{#रनिवृत्तिः#} ‘{%apavarga%} is freedom from transmigration as the re-
<div n="lb">sult of clear discrimination’). Final beatitude being promised
<div n="lb">by each philosophy as the reward of a correct knowledge
<div n="lb">of its principles, the mode of attaining it differs, of course,
<div n="lb">with the doctrine; e. g. {%Nyāya Sūtr.:%} {#दुःखजन्मप्रवृत्तिदोष-#}
<div n="lb">{#मिथ्याज्ञानानामुत्तरोत्तरापाये तदनन्तरापायादपवर्गः#} ‘final
<div n="lb">beatitude takes place after the consecutive annihilation of
<div n="lb">wrong knowledge which is conducive to the annihilation of
<div n="lb">defects (i. e. passions, love, hatred &c.) and so on succes-
<div n="lb">sively to that of exertion (rightful or wrong), of re-birth
<div n="lb">and of pain; the topics of knowledge being in the {%Nyāya%}
<div n="lb">‘Soul, body, organs of sensation, objects of sense, intel-
<div n="lb">lect, imagination, exertion, defects as passions &c., the
<div n="lb">state of the soul after death or re-birth, retribution, pain
<div n="lb">and final beatitude’ ({#आत्मशरीरेन्द्रियार्थबुद्धिमनःप्रवृत्तिदो-#}
<div n="lb">{#षप्रेत्यभावफलदुःखापवर्गास्तु प्रमेयम्#}); for the topics of the
<div n="lb">{%Vaiśeṣika%} comp. {#पदार्थ;#} {%Sāṅkhya Kār.:%} {#ज्ञानेन चापवर्गः#}
<div n="lb">‘through knowledge i. e. of the twenty-five Sāṅkhya prin-
<div n="lb">ciples comes final beatitude (comp. {#तत्त्व#}); a comm. on the
<div n="lb">{%Yoga:%} {#भोक्तुः स्वरूपावधारणमपवर्गः#} ‘final beatitude is com-
<div n="lb">prehending the real nature of him who enjoys, i. e. of
<div n="lb">Purusha or Spirit’. <sup>3</sup> (In the Vaiśeṣika philosophy.)
<div n="lb">Speedy destruction, speedy cessation; in the {%Kaṇāda Sūtra:%}
<div n="lb">{#‘गुणस्य सतोऽपवर्गः कर्मभिः साधर्म्यम्’#} which according to
<div n="lb">the {%Upaskāra%} of {%Śaṅkara%} means: ‘the speedy cessation of
<div n="lb">a quality of what exists (does not come under the category
<div n="lb">of action, but) has (only) properties similar to those of
<div n="lb">actions’ ({%Śaṅk.%} {#अपवर्ग आशुनाशः । स च गुणत्वेऽपि द्वित्वा-#}
<div n="lb">{#दिवदाशुभाविनाशकसंनिपाताधीन इति कर्मभिः साधर्म्यमा-#}
<div n="lb">{#त्रमस्य न तु कर्मत्वमेव ।#}). <sup>4</sup> The place or region where the
<div n="lb">final deliverance is obtained; e. g. in the verse of the {%Bhāgav.%}
<div n="lb">{%Purāṇa:%} {#सूर्येण हि विभज्यन्ते दिशः खं द्यौर्मही भिदा । स्वर्गा-#}
<div n="lb">{#पवर्गौ नरका रसौकांसि च सर्वशः#} (comm. {#स्वर्गापवर्गौ भोग-#}
<div n="lb">{#मोक्षदेशौ#}). <sup>5</sup> A gift, a donation; e. g. {#न तेऽपवर्गः सुकृता-#}
<div n="lb">{#द्विनाकृतस्तथा यथान्येषु वरेषु मानद । वरं वृणे…#} <sup>6</sup> Re-
<div n="lb">striction of a general rule (according to {%Suśruta's%} definition:
<div n="lb">{#अभिव्याप्यापकर्षणमपवर्गः#}). E. {#वृज्#} with {#अप,#} kṛt aff. {#घञ्#}
<div n="lb">lit. ‘leaving, abandoning’; comp. {#अपवर्जन#}.

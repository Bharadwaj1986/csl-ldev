{#अप्राणिन्#}¦ Tatpur. m. f. n. ({#-णी-णिनी-णि#}) Inanimate, lifeless;
<div n="lb">e. g. in {%Manu:%} {#अप्राणिभिर्यत्क्रियते तल्लोके द्यूतमुच्यते#}. (As in
<div n="lb">{%Pāṇini'%}s {%Sūtras%} the neg. {#अ#} is sometimes used in compo-
<div n="lb">sition with a noun, when in ordinary writing it would be
<div n="lb">more correct to employ the uncompounded {#न,#} as referring
<div n="lb">to the verb of the sentence, the interpretation of the word

<div n="lb">rule itself becomes doubtful; the {%Kāśikā%} takes the Sūtra as
<div n="lb">if it ended: {#न प्राणिषु;#} {%Patanjali%} is less positive, for he ex-
<div n="lb">claims, after showing the possibility of either acceptation:
<div n="lb">{#किं पुनरत्रार्थसतत्त्वम् । देवा एतज्ज्ञातुमर्हन्ति;#} but {%Kaiyyaṭa%}
<div n="lb">seems to think that if the gods {%must%} know it, the Paṇḍits may

<div n="lb">{#क्यत्वे वाक्यभेदाश्रयणस्यायुक्तत्वात् । प्रसज्यप्रतिषेधे हि वाक्यभे-#}

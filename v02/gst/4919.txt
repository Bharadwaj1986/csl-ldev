{#अपविद्ध#}¦ Tatpur. m. f. n. ({#-द्धः-द्धा-द्धम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Rejected, discarded,
<div n="lb">disowned; {#अपविद्धः पुत्रः#} is, in law, a son who having
<div n="lb">been disowned by his natural parents or, in the case of the
<div n="lb">death of either of them, having been disowned by the sur-
<div n="lb">viving parent, is adopted by another; one of the twelve
<div n="lb">kinds of adopted sons, comp. s. v. {#अन्यवीजज;#} also said of
<div n="lb">a girl, e. g. in the {%Bhāgav. Pur.%} {#कण्डोः प्रम्लोचया लब्धा कन्या#}
<div n="lb">{#कमललोचना । तां चापविद्धां जगृहुर्भूरुहा नृपनन्दनाः#} (comm.
<div n="lb">{#अपविद्धां त्यक्ताम्#}). <sup>2</sup> Abject, contemptible, e. g. (Rudra speaks
<div n="lb">to Viṣṇu in the {%Bhāgav. Pur.%}) {#यदि रचितधियं माविद्यलोको#}
<div n="lb">{#ऽपविद्धं जपति न गणये तत्त्वत्परानुग्रहेण#} (comm. {#अपविद्धमा-#}
<div n="lb">{#चारभ्रष्टम्#}), ‘if the ignorant world declares me whose mind
<div n="lb">is properly framed, to be abject, I do not mind it on ac-
<div n="lb">count of thy paramount grace’; {%Burnouf%} renders this pas-
<div n="lb">sage: ‘si pendant ce temps le monde ignorant m'appelle
<div n="lb">avec mépris contempteur des lois &c.’. <sup>3</sup> Disabled, broken
<div n="lb">(lit. or fig.), e. g. {#गर्गाणां वाहनमपविद्धम्;#} ({%Kaiyyaṭa:%} {#अप-#}
<div n="lb">{#विद्धमिति वहनशक्तिविकलं प्रनष्टमित्यर्थः#}); or in the {%Mālatīm.%}
<div n="lb">{#हृदयमशरणं मे पक्ष्मलाक्ष्याः कटाक्षैरपहृतमपविद्धं पीतमुन्मूलितं#}
<div n="lb">{#च#}. <sup>4</sup> Neglected; (in this sense also in the Buddh. {%Dhamma-%}
<div n="lb">{%pada%} ed. {%Fausböll%} v. 292). E. {#व्यध्,#} with {#अप,#} kṛt aff. {#क्त#}
<div n="lb">and samprasāraṇa of the radical.

{#अप्रतिषेध#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-धः#}) <sup>1</sup> Absence of a prohibition, non-
prohibition, as of a religious act; e. g. in the {%Jaimini Sūtra:%}
{#अप्रतिषेधो वा दर्शनादिडायां स्यात्#}. Comp. also {#पर्युदास#}.
<sup>2</sup> Absence of negation or contradiction, non-denying (as of
an argument alleged); e. g. in the {%Nyāya Sūtras:%} {#शब्दार्थ-#}
{#व्यवस्थानादप्रतिषेधः;#} or {#प्रतिषेधानुपपत्तेश्च प्रतिषेद्धव्याप्रति-#}
{#षेधः;#} or in the {%Vedānta Sūtra:%} {#विज्ञानादिभावे वा तदप्र-#}
{#तिषेधः#}. <sup>3</sup> Non-exception (as to a rule); e. g. in the {%Vārttika%}
to {%Pāṇ.%} VIII. 4. 3.: {#अप्रतिषेधो वा यथा सर्वनामसंज्ञायाम्#}.
<sup>4</sup> A bad or invalid objection (against an argument), used
frequently in this sense in the {%Nyāya Sūtras;%} e. g. {#गन्धत्वा-#}
{#द्यव्यतिरेकाद्गन्धादीनामप्रतिषेधः#} ‘(what is said before) is an
invalid objection, for odours (scents &c.) have no other
nature than that of odour’. <sup>5</sup> A useless exception, an ex-
ception which need not have been made; e. g. when {%Kātyā-%}
{%yana%} criticises the mentioning of the word {#न्युब्ज#} in {%Pāṇini'%}s
rule VII. 3. 61.: {#न्युब्जेः कर्तृत्वादप्रतिषेधः#} ({%Patanjali:%} {#अनर्थकः#}
{#प्रतिषेधोऽप्रतिषेधः#}); or when a successor of {%Kātyāyana%}
criticises the latter for finding fault with {%Pāṇ.%} VI. 4. 110.,
he says of the {#प्रतिषेध#} of {%Kātyāyana%} that it is an {#अप्रतिषेध;#}
and in the same manner he turns into ridicule the criticism
of {%Kātyāyana%} on {%Pāṇ.%} I. 1. 22.: {#घसंज्ञायां नदीतरे प्रतिषेधः#}
by substituting for the latter words {#नदीतरेऽप्रतिषेधः,#}
when {%Patanjali%} gives the same explanation and {%Kaiyyaṭa%}
[Page211-a+ 56]
observes: {#न प्रतिषेध इति प्रतिषेधनक्रिया नञा निषिध्यते ।#}
{#भाष्यकारस्तु वस्त्वर्थं व्याचष्टे#}.--E. {#अ#} neg. and {#प्रतिषेध#}.

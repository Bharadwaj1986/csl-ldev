{#अन्तर्वावत्#}¦ I. Tatpur. m. f. n. ({#-वन्-वन्ती-वत्#}) (ved.) Con-
cealed, hidden; {#अन्तर्वावदकृणोज्ज्योतिषा तमः#} ‘he has hidden
darkness with light’. E. {#वा,#} in the intens., with {#अन्तर्,#}
kṛt aff. {#शतृ#}.
<div n="P">II. m. f. n. ({#-वान्-वती-वत्#}) (ved.) Endowed with trea-
sures or with sons, cattle &c. in its interior; {#अन्तर्वावत्क्षयं#}
{#दधे#} ‘he possesses a dwelling abounding internally with
treasures or with sons &c.’. E. {#अन्तर्वा,#} taddh. aff. {#मतुप्;#}
Sāyaṇa explains {#अन्तर्वा#} as meaning liter. ‘going within,
[Page126-b+ 56]
being within, belonging to’ and thus assuming the sense of
treasure &c.; he makes it optional, however, to consider
in the latter instance {#अन्तर्वावत्#} as representing also {#अन्त-#}
{#र्वावद्,#} when the word would come from {#अन्तर्#} and {#वावद्#}
‘speaking much’ the intens. of {#वद्,#} kṛt aff. {#क्विप्,#} the
sense remaining the same, but the word being then a
Tatpurusha.

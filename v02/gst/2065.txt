{#अध्यवसान#}¦ Tatpur. n. ({#-नम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Determining, ascertaining. <sup>2</sup> Effort,
<div n="lb">energy. <sup>3</sup> (In rhetoric.) The simple enunciation of elliptic
<div n="lb">or figurative speech without qualifying it by epithets &c.,
<div n="lb">as it were, leaving it on its own effort; such sentences as
<div n="lb">{#श्वेतो धावति#} ‘the white (horse) gallops’, {#कुन्ताः प्रविशन्ति#}
<div n="lb">‘(men with) lances enter’ (see also under {#अजहत्स्वार्था#}), or
<div n="lb">{#कलिङ्गः साहसिकः#} ‘the (inhabitant of) Kalinga is impetuous’
<div n="lb">(see also under {#जहत्स्वार्था#}) would be lakṣaṇās or elliptic
<div n="lb">expressions by {#अध्यवसान,#} while {#अश्वः श्वेतो धावति#} the
<div n="lb">white horse gallops, {#एते कुन्ताः प्रविशन्ति#} these (men with)
<div n="lb">lances enter, {#कलिङ्गः पुरुषो युध्यते#} the man (inhabitant of)
<div n="lb">Kalinga fights would be qualified or lakṣaṇās by {#आरोप#}
<div n="lb">q. v. (See also under {#लक्षणा#}.) Or {#मुखं द्वितीयश्चन्द्रः,#} (her)
<div n="lb">face is another moon, would be an {#अतिशयोक्ति#} (q. v.) or
<div n="lb">hyperbole in virtue of {#अध्यवसान,#} the comparison as it
<div n="lb">were resting on its own merit. E. {#सो#} with {#अव#} and {#अधि,#}
<div n="lb">kṛt aff. {#ल्युट्#}.

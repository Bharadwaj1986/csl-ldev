{#अभौतिक#}¦ Tatpur. m. f. n. ({#-कः-की-कम्#}) Not produced by
<div n="lb">the coarse or visible elements, intellectual.--In the
<div n="lb">{%Sāṅkhya philosophy%} {#अभौतिकसर्ग#} is one of the creations or
<div n="lb">evolutions of {%Prakṛti%} or Matter, comprising the evolutions
<div n="lb">called {#लिङ्गसर्ग#} ‘evolution of mind’ and {#भावसर्ग#} ‘evolution
<div n="lb">of mental affections’, the other evolution of {%Prakṛti%} being
<div n="lb">{#भौतिकसर्ग#} or {#भूतसर्ग,#} produced by the coarse elements and
<div n="lb">comprising eight classes of celestial beings (see {#ब्राह्म, प्रा-#}
<div n="lb">{#जापत्य, सौम्य, ऐन्द्र, गान्धर्व, याक्ष, राक्षस, पैशाच#})--in
<div n="lb">whom prevails the quality {#सत्त्व#}--, five species of brute
<div n="lb">creation (tame animals, wild animals, birds, reptiles and
<div n="lb">immovable substances)--in whom prevails the quality
<div n="lb">{#तमस्#}--, and the species man, with the predominant quality
<div n="lb">{#रजस्;#} ‘both, the {#अभौतिक#}--and the {#भौतिक#}--evolutions,
<div n="lb">comprise therefore sixteen evolutions of Matter’. [This is, in
<div n="lb">my opinion, the bearing of {%Gaudapāda'%}s concluding remark
<div n="lb">on the {%Sāṅkhya-Kār.%} v. 54: {#एवमभौतिकः सर्गो लिङ्गसर्गो भा-#}
<div n="lb">{#वसर्गो#}--cfr. v. 52--{#भूतसर्गो दैवमानुषतैर्यग्योन#}--cfr. v. 53--
<div n="lb">{#इत्येष प्रधानकृतः षोडशसर्गः#}]. E. {#अ#} neg. and {#भौतिक#}.

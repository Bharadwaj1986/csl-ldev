{#अन्योऽन्य#}¦ (usually written {#अन्योन्य#}) Tatpur. (see {#सर्वनामन्#})
<div n="lb">1. m. f. n. ({#-न्यः-न्या-न्यम्,#} not. {#अन्योन्यद्;#} in the other cases
<div n="lb">it follows the declension of {#अन्य#} q. v.) One-another, mutual.--
<div n="lb"><sup>a.</sup> The verb in connexion with {#अन्योन्य#} stands (acc. to Pāṇ.) in
<div n="lb">the Parasmaipada, not in the Ātmanepada; e. g. {#अन्योन्यस्य#}
<div n="lb">{#व्यतिपुनन्ति, अन्योन्यस्य व्यतिलुनन्ति#}.--<sup>b.</sup> If {#अन्योन्य#} refers
<div n="lb">to the subject of the sentence and the subject is a mascu-
<div n="lb">line, it is possible to consider it as consisting of two dis-
<div n="lb">tinct not compound words {#अन्य,#} the former being a nomi-
<div n="lb">native as attribute to the subject, while the latter assumes
<div n="lb">the case required by the sentence (see the Etym.); e. g.
<div n="lb">{#अन्योऽन्यमाह्वयन्तौ तु मत्ताविव महागजौ;#} or {#तेऽन्योन्यमभि-#}
<div n="lb">{#संसृत्य क्षिपन्तो नामभिर्मिथः;#} or {#…कपिराक्षसाः । अन्योऽन्येना-#}
<div n="lb">{#भ्यसूयन्त विमर्दमसहन्त च;#} or in sentences in which the gerond
<div n="lb">is used elliptically; e. g. {#ताभ्यामन्योऽन्यमासाद्य समवाप्यत सं-#}
<div n="lb">{#शमः#}.--<sup>c.</sup> There are however many instances in which
<div n="lb">{#अन्योन्य#} does not refer to the subject, or in which it is the
<div n="lb">attribute of a subject which is not a masculine and there-
<div n="lb">fore cannot be looked upon otherwise than as a compound;
<div n="lb">e. g. {#अन्योन्येन कृतम्;#} or {#अन्योन्यस्मै दत्तम्;#} or {#अन्योन्येषां पु-#}
<div n="lb">{#ष्करैरामृशन्तो दानोद्भेदादुच्चकैर्भुग्नवालाः;#} or as an attribute
<div n="lb">of a neuter {#प्रीत्यप्रीतिविषादाद्यैर्गुणानामन्योन्यं वैधर्म्यम्;#} and
<div n="lb">the same must be observed of all the words in which {#अन्योन्य#}
<div n="lb">is the first part of a compound; comp. e. g. the following
<div n="lb">articles and {#अन्योन्यतस्#}. <sup>d.</sup> {#अन्योन्यम्#} is frequently used ad-
<div n="lb">verbially, e. g. {#यद्यप्यात्मनामन्योन्यं भेदवाक्यवदभेदवाक्यान्यपि#}
<div n="lb">{#सन्ति तथापि नाद्वैतं नात्यन्तमभेदः।--अन्योन्यम्#} or {#अन्योन्याम्#}
<div n="lb">are used optionally in the sense of an accusative, if the
<div n="lb">subject to which they refer is either a feminine or a neuter;
<div n="lb">e. g. {#अन्योन्यमिमे ब्राह्मण्यौ भोजयतः#} or--{#अन्योन्यां भोजयतः;#}
<div n="lb">{#अन्योन्यमिमे ब्राह्मणकुले भोजयतः#} or--{#अन्योन्यां भोजयतः;#}
<div n="lb">these are the instances given by Patanjali on the Vārttika
<div n="lb">12 to Pāṇ. VIII. 1. 12, and because they show either word
<div n="lb">as representing in meaning the accus. of {#अन्योन्य,#} some
<div n="lb">grammarians (‘of the East’, according to the Prauḍhama-
<div n="lb">noramā on the same rule) contend that the option is re-
<div n="lb">stricted to the meaning of this case, but that {#अन्योन्यम्#} alone
<div n="lb">is admissible when expressing adverbially the sense of the
<div n="lb">other cases. Others, on the contrary, maintain that the quoted
<div n="lb">words are intended by Patanjali merely as an instance and
<div n="lb">that the option extends also to sentences in which {#अन्योन्यम्#}
<div n="lb">would imply the sense of an instrumental &c. Bhaṭṭoji, re-
<div n="lb">ferring in the Siddh. Kaum. on the quoted rule to Patanjali's
<div n="lb">gloss on the 5th Bhāṣyakārikā to Pāṇ. IV. 1. 3, sides with
<div n="lb">the former opinion. (Prauḍham. {#यदपि तृतीयादिषु पुंवदिति#}
<div n="lb">{#प्राचो ग्रन्थं व्याचक्षाणा आहुः । तृतीयादिषु पुंवद्भावविशेषः ।#}
<div n="lb">{#तेन टाप् न । अत्र तृतीयादिष्वविवक्षितम् । द्वितीयायामपि पुं-#}
[Page148-b+ 56]
<div n="lb">{#वत्त्वस्येष्टत्वादिति । तच्चिन्त्यम् । पुंवत्त्वं विशेष इति विशेषाभिधा-#}
<div n="lb">{#नमविवक्षितमिति च स्वोक्तेः । परस्परव्याधात् । मूलकृता पुंव-#}
<div n="lb">{#द्भावस्यास्पृष्टत्वात् । तद्ग्रन्थस्याञ्जस्येनैव व्याख्यातत्वाच्चेति दिक् ॥#});
<div n="lb">Jayamangala, however (in the printed ed. of the Bhaṭṭikāvya)
<div n="lb">differs materially from either of the quoted authorities; for in
<div n="lb">his comment on Bhaṭṭik. 8. 6. he not merely quotes apparently
<div n="lb">a Vārttika which varies in important points from that of Pa-
<div n="lb">tanjali, but justifies {#अन्योन्यम्#} of the text by adding: {#स्त्रीनपुं-#}
<div n="lb">{#सकयोरामभाव इति वक्तव्यम्#} ‘if the subject is a feminine or a
<div n="lb">neuter, the form {#अन्योन्याम्#} is not admissible’ (the subject of
<div n="lb">the commented sentence being {#उदन्वान्#} and the femin. {#रा-#}
<div n="lb">{#क्षसी#}); yet the correctness of the printed text alluded to is
<div n="lb">very doubtful; for the E. I. H. Ms. 544 reads this comm. thus:
<div n="lb">{#अन्योन्यमित्यादि । अन्योन्यमित्यस्येत्यर्थः । कर्मव्यतीहारे सर्वना-#}
<div n="lb">{#म्नी द्विर्भवतः । समासवच्च बहुलम् । यदा समासवत्तदा पूर्वप-#}
<div n="lb">{#दस्य प्रथमैकवचनम् । उत्तरपदस्याम्भाव इति वाच्यम् । अन्यो-#}
<div n="lb">{#न्यसंबन्धिभिः शब्दैः#} &c. &c. (Comp. also the comm. on Bhaṭṭ.
<div n="lb">1. 21.)--As regards the classical literature there can be no
<div n="lb">doubt that the adverbial use of {#अन्योन्यम्#} prevails almost ex-
<div n="lb">clusively over that of {#अन्योन्याम्#}. <sup>e.</sup> The Gaṇaratnamaho-
<div n="lb">dadhi (which mentions {#अन्योन्य#} among the {#सर्वनामन्#} q. v.) gives
<div n="lb">{#अन्योन्यस्य#} in the Gaṇa {#चादि,#} imparting to the genitive the
<div n="lb">value of an adverb or that of {#अन्योन्यम्;#} but the instance,
<div n="lb">Vardhamāna gives in his comm. on this work, {#‘अन्योन्यस्य#}
<div n="lb">{#स्मरन्ति’#} is not happy, as the genitive could be the legitimate
<div n="lb">government of {#स्मरन्ति; ‘अन्योन्यस्य भोजयन्ति’,#} the instance
<div n="lb">of the Kāśikā to Pāṇ. VIII. 1. 12. Vārtt. 11; {#‘अन्योन्यस्य व्य-#}
<div n="lb">{#तिपुनन्ति’#} of Patanjali to Pāṇ. III. 1. 16. and {#‘अन्योन्यस्य#}
<div n="lb">{#व्यतिलुनन्ति’#} of the Kāś. to the same rule, seem more to the
<div n="lb">purpose; but the occurence of the genitive in the sense of
<div n="lb">other cases is, in general, rare.--Comp. {#इतरेतर#} and {#परस्पर#}.
<div n="P">2. n. ({#-न्यम्#}) (In Rhetoric.) An {#अलंकार#} q. v. or mode
<div n="lb">of elegant expression; viz. conveying the mutual relation
<div n="lb">or intimacy of two objects by representing them as the
<div n="lb">mutual cause of the same action ({#‘अर्थयोरेकक्रियामुखेन#}
<div n="lb">{#परस्परं कारणत्वे सत्यन्योन्यं नामालंकारः’#}), e. g. ‘she shineth
<div n="lb">through thee and thou shinest through her, like as the
<div n="lb">moon shineth through the night and the night shineth through
<div n="lb">the moon’. E. {#अन्यस्#} and {#अन्य#}. [The oldest grammatical
<div n="lb">authorities do not consider {#अन्योन्य#} as a compound, but
<div n="lb">take {#अन्योन्येन#} and other cases as consisting of the two dis-
<div n="lb">tinct words: the nomin. of {#अन्य#} and the instrum. of {#अन्य#}
<div n="lb">&c.; see Vārtt. 11 to Pāṇ. VIII. 1. 12. and the remark of
<div n="lb">Kaiyyaṭa: {#तत्र बहुलग्रहणादन्योन्यशब्दे समासवद्भावाभावा-#}
<div n="lb">{#त्सुब्लुङ्ग भवति । नापि समासान्तोदात्तत्वमित्याम्रेडितानुदा-#}
<div n="lb">{#त्तत्वं भवति#} (comp. Pāṇ. VIII. 1. 3); but it results from the
<div n="lb">foregoing statement that this etym. cannot be entertained.
<div n="lb">Jayamangala on the Bhaṭṭikāvya 17. 8. appears to consider
<div n="lb">{#अन्योन्य#} not as a Tatpur., but as Karmadh. (in explaining
<div n="lb">{#अन्योन्यम्#} by {#‘अन्यस्य अन्यस्य’#}), probably in thinking of Pāṇ.
<div n="lb">VIII. 1. 11. which applies to VIII. 1. 12; but the possibility
<div n="lb">of this application is removed by the quoted words of
<div n="lb">Kaiyyaṭa and by the general remark he appends to the
<div n="lb">Vārttikas of VIII. 1. 12, viz. {#वार्तिकेषु कर्मधारयवदिति न#}
<div n="lb">{#संबध्यत इति सुब्लोपादि न भवति#}. A modern theory takes
<div n="lb">{#अन्योन्य#} for a compound (it does not say which), but calls
[Page149-a+ 56]
<div n="lb">the former part of this word a ‘benumbed’ nominative. It
<div n="lb">seems, however, that the sense and accent of the word (it is
<div n="lb">udātta on the second syllable) decide in the first place for
<div n="lb">its being a Tatpur., and secondly its form, that it belongs
<div n="lb">to that class of compounds in which the former part has
<div n="lb">received a further developement by means of an affix {#स्,#}
<div n="lb">the function of which {#स्#} apparently consists in producing a
<div n="lb">stronger relation between the two parts of the compound and
<div n="lb">consequently a more intense unity of notion, than would be
<div n="lb">expressed without this {#स्;#} comp. e. g. the derivatives of
<div n="lb">{#सम्-कृ#} and {#संस्-कृ, परि-कृ#} and {#परिष्-कृ, अप-कॄ#} and {#अपस्-#}
<div n="lb">{#कॄ; आ-चर्य#} and {#आश्-चर्य, अप-कर#} and {#अपस्-कर#} &c. and
<div n="lb">such words as {#हरिश्-चन्द्र, अजस्-तुन्द, बृहस्-पति, प्रायश्-#}
<div n="lb">{#चित्ति#} &c. &c. (comp. Pāṇ. VI. 1. 135-157.) which resemble closely
<div n="lb">in formation {#अन्योऽन्य#} (and the synon. {#परस्पर#}); the accent of
<div n="lb">this word would then fall under the category of Pāṇ. VI. 2. 2.]

{#अभोज्यान्न#}¦ Bahuvr. m. f. n. ({#-न्नः-न्ना-न्नम्#}) One whose food
<div n="lb">must not be eaten (because eating it makes impure or brings
<div n="lb">misfortune); e. g. {%Manu:%} {#य एते#} (those mentioned in the
<div n="lb">preceding verses) {#ऽन्ये त्वभोज्यान्नाः क्रमशः परिकीर्तिताः ।#}
<div n="lb">{#तेषां त्वगस्थिरोमाणि वदन्त्यन्नं मनीषिणः#} ‘…their food, the
<div n="lb">wise say, is (like) skin, bones and hair’. Such persons
<div n="lb">are, for instance, an insane man, a wrathful person, a
<div n="lb">thief, a public singer, a carpenter, a eunuch, a woman in
<div n="lb">her courses or in childbed, a backbiter, a tailor, a black-
<div n="lb">smith, a physician, a libidinous woman, a usurer &c.; see
<div n="lb">{%Manu%} 4. 205-221.; {%Yājnav.%} 1. 160-168.--One who has un-
<div n="lb">knowingly eaten the food of such persons must fast during
<div n="lb">three days; but, having eaten it knowingly he must per-
<div n="lb">form the penance called {%Kṛṣchhra,%} as if he had tasted
<div n="lb">seminal impurity, ordure or urine; according to {%Manu%} 4. 222.
<div n="lb">In another verse, however, {%Manu%} (11. 152.) is satisfied, if
<div n="lb">such a sinner drinks barley gruel for seven days and nights
[Page305-b+ 56]
<div n="lb">(see the quot. s. v. {#अभोज्य#}). Comp. {%Raghunand.%} I. p. 317 ff.
<div n="lb">E. {#अभोज्य#} and {#अन्न#}.

{#अनुलोम#}¦ I. Tatpur. 1. m. f. n. ({#-मः-मा-मम्#}) In natural
order or direction, regular, successive (liter. with the hair),
the reverse of {#प्रतिलोम;#} comp. e. g. {#प्रतिलोमानुलोमपाद-#}
{#यमक#}.
<div n="P">2. m. ({#-मः#}) <sup>1</sup> A proper name; his descendants are called
{#अनुलोमाः#} or {#आनुलोमयः#} (plur. of {#आनुलोमि#}); according
to another authority, however, the name of the descendants
ought to be {#अनुलोमानः#} or {#आनुलोमयः#} (plur. of {#आनुलोमि#}).
<sup>2</sup> (In vaidik grammar; scil. {#अन्वक्षरसन्धि#}) One of the two
kinds of the Sandhi called {#अन्वक्षरसन्धि#} q. v., viz. when a
final vowel or the last syllable of {#एषः, स्यः#} or {#सः#} is fol-
lowed by a consonant (of a new word; the case in the latter
three instances being similar to those of the meeting between
a final vowel and a beginning consonant, since their Visarga
is dropped). Compare {#प्रतिलोम#}.
<div n="P">3. m. plur. ({#-माः#}) scil. {#वर्णाः;#} mixed tribes or castes;
for the special meaning, in which this word is used, see
{#अनुलोमज#}.
<div n="P">4. f. ({#-मा#}) A woman of a caste inferior to that of the
man; e. g. a Kṣatriyā woman when spoken of in reference
to a Brāhmaṇa man. Comp. {#अनुलोमज#}.
<div n="P">[5. (In Buddhistic literature the word {#अनुलोम#} expresses
the regular order in which certain ascetic rites are to be
performed; e. g. the kasinānuloma consists of the eight
first kasinas in their regular order, the dhyānānuloma of
the four successive dhyānas.]
<div n="P">II. Avyayībh. ({#-मम्#}) In natural order, regularly, suc-
cessively. E. {#अनु#} and {#लोमन्,#} samāsānta aff. {#अच्#}.

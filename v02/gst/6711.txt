{#अभ्यवहार्य#}¦ Tatpur. 1. m. f. n. ({#-र्यः-र्या-र्यम्#}) Fit to be taken
<div n="lb">as food (solid or liquid); e. g. {%Vārtt.%} 1. to {%Pāṇ.%} VII. 3. 69.:
<div n="lb">{#भोज्यमभ्यवहार्यमिति वक्तव्यम्,#} with which words {%Kātyāyana%}
<div n="lb">criticizes {%Pāṇini%} for having either restricted the sense of
<div n="lb">{#भोज्य#} to that of {#भक्ष्य#} (‘fit to serve as solid food’) or used
<div n="lb">{#भक्ष्य#} in the general acceptation of {#अभ्यवहार्य#}. {%Patanjali,%} it
[Page311-a+ 56]
<div n="lb">is true, defends {%Pāṇini%} (as he frequently sides with {%Pāṇini%}
<div n="lb">against the hypercriticisms of {%Kātyāyana%}) also on this oc-
<div n="lb">casion by alleging {#अब्भक्ष#} and {#वायुभक्ष#} in evidence that {#भक्ष्#}
<div n="lb">(and, impliedly, that {#भक्ष्य#}) are used in a general sense;
<div n="lb">but on another occasion when he shows that there are
<div n="lb">words the bearing of which in the classical language can
<div n="lb">merely be established from the context in which they occur
<div n="lb">in the vaidik literature, and not be made out otherwise (see
<div n="lb">{#एकपद#}), he gives these very instances as a proof of his
<div n="lb">latter assertion, and admitting therefore that they belong to
<div n="lb">this exceptional category, corroborates thus involuntarily
<div n="lb">the view of {%Kātyāy.%} that {#भक्ष्#} cannot be applied to the word
<div n="lb">{#अप्#} in a {%general sense,%} and much less to any other word
<div n="lb">having the sense of a liquid substance ({%Patanj. Introd.:%}
<div n="lb">{#अथवा सन्त्येकपदान्यप्यवधारणानि तद्यथा । अब्भक्षो वायुभक्ष#}
<div n="lb">{#इति । अप एव भक्षयति वायुमेव भक्षयतीति गम्यते । एवमि-#}
<div n="lb">{#हापि सिद्ध एव न साध्य इति;#} {%Kaiyy.:%} {#एवशब्दप्रयोगे द्विपद-#}
<div n="lb">{#मवधारणम् । द्योतकत्वेन एवशब्दस्यापेक्षणात् । यदा तु द्योत-#}
<div n="lb">{#कमन्तरेण सामर्थ्यादवधारणं गम्यते तदा तदेकपदमित्युच्यते#}
<div n="lb">{#तत्र सर्व एवापो भक्षयन्तीत्यब्भक्षश्रुतिः सामर्थ्यान्नियममवगम-#}
<div n="lb">{#यति । अप एवेति । इहापि नित्यानित्यव्यतिरेकेण राश्यन्तरा-#}
<div n="lb">{#भावात्सिद्धशब्दोपादानान्नियमोऽवगम्यते#}). But as {#भक्ष्#} {%is%}
<div n="lb">applied to {#अप्,#} in the vaidik language, and as it seems
<div n="lb">strange that {%Pāṇini%} should have erred in the use of so
<div n="lb">common words as {#भोज्य#} or {#भक्ष्य,#} it is possible also to ob-
<div n="lb">tain another result from this discussion of {%Patanjali,%} which
<div n="lb">would reconcile the observations he makes in his Introd.,
<div n="lb">with those he has appended to the quoted Sūtra, a result
<div n="lb">supported by many {%Sūtras%} of {%Pāṇini,%} viz. that {%Kātyāyana,%}
<div n="lb">far from being a contemporary of {%Pāṇini,%} as is stated in
<div n="lb">one of the ghost stories of the {%Kathāsaritsāgara,%} has lived,
<div n="lb">on the contrary, at another period, and that many {%Vārttikas%}
<div n="lb">of his must not be looked upon {%by us%} as impugning the ac-
<div n="lb">curacy of {%Pāṇini'%}s rules at the time when this grammarian
<div n="lb">wrote, but as commenting upon, extending and restricting
<div n="lb">the validity of these rules at a probably much later period
<div n="lb">of the Sanskṛt literature. See my Preface to the {%Mānava-%}
<div n="lb">{%Kalpa-Sūtras.%}

<div n="lb">{#वहार्यं भक्षशब्देनोच्यते#}. E. {#हृ#} with {#अव#} and {#अभि,#} kṛtya aff. {#ण्यत्#}.

{#अन्तर्यामिन्#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-मी#}) <sup>1</sup> (In the Upan. and the oldest
Vedānta phil.) The supreme Soul as the inward regulator
of all the phenomena of the material and intellectual world,
in this and in a future life: {#‘य इमं च लोकं परं च लोकं#}
{#सर्वाणि च भूतान्यन्तरो यमयति’; ‘अधिदैवतमधिलोकमधि-#}
{#वेदमधियज्ञमधिभूतमध्यात्मं च कश्चिदन्तरवस्थितो यमयिता-#}
{#न्तर्यामी’;#} not merely ‘the individual Soul of a divinity, of
a living being &c.’, but the same as the {#परमात्मन्; ‘अन्तर्याम्य-#}
{#धिदैवादिषु तद्धर्मव्यपदेशात्’…‘तस्मात्परमात्मैवान्तर्यामी’#}.--
<sup>2</sup> (In the later Vedānta.) Brahman (m.) as creator of the
world when under the influence of the first Gunā or of the
cosmical quality of {#सत्त्व#} ‘goodness’; as such however, he
is not the purest form of Brahman, but inferior to Īśa who
is freed of all cosmical qualities. <sup>3</sup> (In the Vedānta applied to
the doctrine of the sects.) <sup>a.</sup> Viṣṇu or Purushottama when be-
coming human or individual Soul; e. g. {#अन्तः प्रविश्य भूतानि#}
{#यो बिभर्त्यात्मकेतुभिः । अन्तर्यामीश्वरः साक्षात्पातु नो यद्वशे#}
{#स्फुटम् ॥;#} <sup>b.</sup> Śiva. E. {#अन्तर्#} and {#यामिन्#}.

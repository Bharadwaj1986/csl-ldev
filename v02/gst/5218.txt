{#अपुत्र#}¦ I. Tatpur. m. ({#-त्रः#}) One who is not a son. E. {#अ#}
<div n="lb">neg. and {#पुत्र#}.
<div n="P">II. Bahuvr. m. f. ({#-त्रः-त्रा#}) Sonless; one who has either
<div n="lb">had no son or lost his (her) son or sons ({#अपुत्रोऽजातपुत्रो मृत-#}
<div n="lb">{#पुत्रो वा#}) and is legally held to adopt one ({%Atri:%} {#अपुत्रेणैव#}
<div n="lb">{#कर्तव्यः पुत्रप्रतिनिधिः सदा । पिण्डोदकक्रियाहेतोर्यस्मात्तस्मा-#}
<div n="lb">{#त्प्रयत्नतः#}). {%Manu%} uses the term also impliedly of a man
<div n="lb">who has no son by a woman of the three first classes,
<div n="lb">although he may have a son by a Śūdrā woman, as re-
<div n="lb">sults from this verse: {#यद्यपि स्यात्तु सत्पुत्रो यद्यपुत्रोऽपि वा#}
<div n="lb">{#भवेत् । नाधिकं दशमाद्दद्याच्छूद्रापुत्राय धर्मतः#} (and {%Vṛ-%}
<div n="lb">{%haspati%} employs in a similar manner the term {#अनपत्य,#} viz.
<div n="lb">{#अनपत्यस्य शुश्रूषुर्गुणवाञ्छूद्रयोनिजः । लभेताजीवनं शेषं सपि-#}
<div n="lb">{#ण्डाः समवाप्नुयुः#}); when {%Jīmūtavāhana%} moreover observes
<div n="lb">that the son of a Śūdrā in this verse applies to the legiti-
<div n="lb">mate son, the Pāraśava (q. v.) being the son of a Brāhmana
<div n="lb">by a Śūdrā not wedded to him ({#यच्चाह मनुः । यं ब्राह्मणस्तु#}
<div n="lb">{#शूद्रायां#} &c. {%Manu%} 9. 178. {#। तदपरिणीतशूद्रासुताभिप्रायम्;#}
<div n="lb">contrary to {%Kullūka%} who defines him as {#परिणीतायामेव#}
<div n="lb">{#शूद्रायां ब्राह्मणः कामाद्यं पुत्रं जनयेत्#} &c.).--A wife who
<div n="lb">has no son must be approached for the sake of getting one
<div n="lb">by her brother-in-law or by one of her relatives of the
<div n="lb">Sapiṇḍa class.--Although {%Likhita%} places a man who has
<div n="lb">no son in the same category with an impious man, a usurer
[Page196-a+ 56]
<div n="lb">and a Śūdra, when he inflicts a fast of three days on any
<div n="lb">one who eats the food of such a person ({#भुक्त्वा वार्धुषिक-#}
<div n="lb">{#स्यान्नमव्रतस्यासुतस्य च । शूद्रस्य च तथा भुक्त्वा त्रिरात्रं स्याद-#}
<div n="lb">{#भोजनम्#}), the present edition of {%Pāṇini%} gives {#अपुत्र#} in a
<div n="lb">{%Gaṇa%} (to VIII. 1. 67.) as a {%honorific%} term when it is the first
<div n="lb">part of a {%Karmadh.%} (the latter part of such a compound
<div n="lb">being anudātta); but on the other hand, as {#अपुत्र#} is one of
<div n="lb">the thirty-four nativities from the knowledge of which
<div n="lb">{%Śākyamuni%} derives a special name (comp. {#चतुस्त्रिंशज्जात-#}
<div n="lb">{#कज्ञ#}), it would seem that the word in this sense belongs
<div n="lb">rather to the Buddhistic than to the Brāhmanic literature;
<div n="lb">compare e. g. the Pāli word aputtakaseṭṭhī, in {%Fansböll'%}s
<div n="lb">{%Dhammapadam%} p. 415. The {%Kāśikā%} does not mention {#अपुत्र#}
<div n="lb">in the {%Gaṇa%} alluded to. E. {#अ#} priv. and {#पुत्र#}.

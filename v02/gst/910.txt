{#अजित#}¦ Tatpur. I. m. f. n. ({#-तः-ता-तम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Unconquered. <sup>2</sup> Un-
excelled, unsurpassed.
[Page026-b+ 56]
<div n="P">II. m. ({#-तः#}) <sup>1</sup> The proper name of several gods and per-
sons, viz. <sup>a.</sup> Viṣṇu. <sup>b.</sup> Śiva. <sup>c.</sup> One of the seven Ṛṣis
who preside during the reign of the fourteenth Manu. <sup>d.</sup> A
name of Maitreya or a future Buddha. <sup>e.</sup> The second of
the Arhats or Jaina saints of the present Avasarpiṇī,
a descendant of Ikṣvāku and a son of Jitaśatru by Vijayā.
<sup>f.</sup> The attendant of Suvidhi or the ninth Jaina Arhat of the
present Avasarpiṇī. <sup>2</sup> The name of a powerful antidote.
<sup>3</sup> A kind of poisonous rat.
<div n="P">III. m. pl. ({#-ताः#}) A particular class of gods in the reign
of the first Manu, the same as the {#जयाः#} in a former life.
E. {#अ#} neg. and {#जित#}.

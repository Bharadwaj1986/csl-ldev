<L>5642<pc>221-a<k1>अप्रीति<k2>अप्रीति
{#अप्रीति#}¦ Tatpur. f. ({#-तिः#}) <sup>1</sup> Absence of affection, aversion
<div n="lb">enmity ({%Mallinātha:%} {#अपरागोऽप्रीतिर्द्वेषः#}). <sup>2</sup> Pain, grief.
[Page221-b+ 56]
<div n="lb">--In the {%Śaṅkhya philosophy%} {#अप्रीति#} ‘pain’ is the essential
<div n="lb">characteristic of the elementary quality {#रजस्#} q. v., the
<div n="lb">middle quality between clearness (goodness) and darkness
<div n="lb">(sinfulness); {%Sāṅkhya Sūtra:%} {#प्रीत्यप्रीतिविषादाद्यैर्गुणानाम-#}
<div n="lb">{#न्योन्यं वैधर्म्यम्;#} {%Gaurapāda%} on the {%S. Kār.:%} {#अप्रीत्यात्मकं रजः;#}
<div n="lb">but the term ‘pain’ is there, according to the commenta-
<div n="lb">tors, a general term which comprises besides its own notion
<div n="lb">that of {%hatred, violence, envy, abuse%} and {%wickedness,%} or various
<div n="lb">kinds of {%grief%} in general: {%Sāṅkhya Chandrikā:%} {#अप्रीतिर्दुःख-#}
<div n="lb">{#मुपलक्षणं प्रद्वेषद्रोहमत्सरनिन्दानिकृतीनाम्;#} {%Vijnānāch.:%} {#रजो#}
<div n="lb">{#ऽपि शोकादिनानाभेदं समासतः दुःखात्मकम्;#} in a similar
<div n="lb">manner {%Manu,%} who names however {#दुःख#} and {#अप्रीति#} as
<div n="lb">two distinct qualities connected with {#रजस्,#} the first, as it
<div n="lb">were, as the quality inherent in it, the second as the effect
<div n="lb">produced by it, while the term {#अप्रीति#} is used by the
<div n="lb">Sāṅkhya writers in either acceptation; {%Manu:%} {#यत्तु दुःखस-#}
<div n="lb">{#मायुक्तमप्रीतिकरमात्मनः । तद्रजः#} &c. E. {#अ#} neg. and {#प्रीति#}.

{#अन्तिक#}¦ I. 1. m. f. n. ({#-कः-का-कम्#}) Near, proximate. Comp.
{#अन्त, अन्तर, अन्तम, अन्ति, अन्तिकतम, अन्तितम, अन्तिय,#}
{#अन्त्य#}.--The words {#नेदीयस्#} and {#नेदिष्ठ#} are considered
as the comparative and superl. of {#अन्तिक#}.--In the dramas
{#जनान्तिकम्#} ‘what is spoken apart to a person so that none else
may hear’ is opposed to {#स्वगतम्#} ‘what is spoken to one's self’.
<div n="P">2. n. ({#-कम्#}) Vicinity, neighbourhood. [{#अन्तिकात्#} forms
Tatpur. compounds with a following kṛt formation in {#क्त#}
(i. e. with a past participle), e. g. {#अन्तिकादागत;#}--such
a word being a compound, because it has but one accent--;
{#अन्तिकम्, अन्तिकेन, अन्तिकात्#} and {#अन्तिके#} are, besides in
their literal acceptations, used like prepositions with a noun
following or preceding in the genit. or ablat.; e. g. {#अन्तिकं#}
{#ग्रामस्य#} or {#ग्रामात्#} ‘near a village’. The correctness of
the restriction enjoined by some grammarians, not to com-
bine the ablat. {#अन्तिकात्#} with a noun in the ablat., is de-
nied by Patanjali and his comm.; comp. the instance s. v.
{#दूर।--अन्तिकात्#} (lit. from the vicinity of) occurs also
in the sense of ‘from’, much in the same manner as the
ablat. {#सकाशात्;#} e. g. {#क्रीणीयाद्यस्त्वपत्यार्थं मातापित्रोर्यम-#}
{#न्तिकात् । स क्रीतकः#} &c.; or {#नापराध्नीदियं किंचिदभ्रश्यत्पत्यु-#}
{#रन्तिकात्#}. NB. Words like {#केशान्तिक, नासान्तिक, ग्रहणा-#}
{#न्तिक, आमरणान्तिक, प्राणान्तिक#} are not compounds of
{#केश, नासा#} &c. and {#अन्तिक,#} but deriv. with taddh. aff. {#ठन्#}
of {#केशान्त, नासान्त#} &c.] E. {#अन्त#} or {#अन्ति,#} taddh. aff. {#ठन्#}.
<div n="P">II. f. ({#-का#}) A fire place, a furnace. Also {#अन्ती#} and
{#अन्दिका#}. E. The native comm. derive it from {#अन्त्,#} kṛt
aff. {#ण्वुल्#} ‘because it connects with itself the cooking uten-
sils’; but though a similar ellipsis is implied by its synon.
{#अधिश्रयणी,#} the same notion might be obtained by con-
necting its etym. with that of {#अन्तिक#}.
<div n="P">III. f. ({#-का#}) The name of a plant (Echites scholaris),
a decoction of which is used for one of the urinary dis-
eases. E. Perhaps from {#अन्ति#} II., denom. of {#अन्त,#} kṛt aff.
[Page128-a+ 56]
{#ण्वुल्,#} the fem. of {#अन्तक;#} compare its synonyme {#शातला#}
(probably from the caus. of {#शद्#}).
<div n="P">IV. f. ({#-का#}) (In theatrical language.) An elder sister,
the same as {#अत्तिका#} of which it seems to be, like {#अर्तिका,#}
a degenerated form.

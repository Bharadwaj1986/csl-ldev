{#अनुनासिक#}¦ Tatpur. m. f. n. ({#-कः-का-कम्#}) (In Grammar.) Uttered
<div n="lb">through the nose, nasal (see also {#रक्त#}), viz. <sup>1</sup> A nasal sound
<div n="lb">in general, it being pronounced through the nose and with
<div n="lb">the appropriate organ of speech, as {#ङ्#} through the nose
<div n="lb">and the throat, {#ञ्#} through the nose and with the palate &c.
<div n="lb"><sup>2</sup> (Conditionally) a vowel or {#य्, व्, ल्;#} in this case the nasal
<div n="lb">nature of the sound is indicated by the sign {#ंँ#}. (N. B. {#य्ंँ#} or
<div n="lb">{#व्ंँ#} represent the value of {#य्#} or {#व्#} preceded by {#म्; ल्ंँ#} that
<div n="lb">of {#ल्#} preceded by {#म्#} or {#न्#}. In the pause a final vowel may
<div n="lb">be anunāsika. A {%short%} anunāsika vowel, when not occurr-
<div n="lb">ing in a pause, represents apparently always the value of
<div n="lb">the same vowel and {#म्#} or {#न्;#} it must be, in general, the
<div n="lb">last vowel sound of the former part of a compound or of
<div n="lb">the word itself, followed by a final sibilant, e. g. {#संँस्कर्ता#}
<div n="lb">instead of {#सम्स्-कर्ता, पुंँश्चली = पुम्स्-चली, तस्मिंँस्त्वा =#}
<div n="lb">{#तस्मिन्स्-त्वा#}. Only a few exceptions of this rule are mentioned
<div n="lb">by the vaidik grammarians, e. g. {#अमिनन्तंँ एवैः,#} when {#अ#} of {#न्त#}
<div n="lb">is anunāsika, although it does neither stand in a pause nor
<div n="lb">represent more than its own sound.--A {%long%} anunāsika
<div n="lb">vowel, when not occurring in a pause, may be generally
<div n="lb">considered as representing the value of its short vowel and
<div n="lb">an original {#न्त्स्#} or of its short vowel and {#न्त्,#} provided {#स्#} or
<div n="lb">its substitute has been preserved; e. g. {#महांँ इन्द्रः = महन्त्स्#}
<div n="lb">{#इन्द्रः, दधन्वांँ यः = दधन्वन्त्स् यः, सर्गांँ इव = सर्गन्त्स् इव#}
<div n="lb">or {#महांँश्चरति = महन्त्स् चरति, दस्यूंँरेकः = दस्युन्त्स् एकः#}.
<div n="lb">The material difference that prevails between the anunāsika
<div n="lb">({#ंँ#}) and the anusvāra ({#ं#}) sound, does not appear to have
<div n="lb">been kept alive beyond the period of the vaidik literature,
<div n="lb">when an interchange between both was not deemed legiti-
<div n="lb">mate; according to the grammarians of the classical period,
<div n="lb">in the latter the anunāsika may be replaced by the anu-
<div n="lb">svāra. The sign of the anunāsika ({#ंँ#}), however, is but
<div n="lb">seldom met with in the ordinary Mss. referring to the post-
<div n="lb">vaidik literature; and from the reasons stated, its use in
<div n="lb">vaidik Mss. is objectionable, when it occurs in words like
<div n="lb">{#यशांँसि, धनूंँषि, विश्वेषांँ हितः#} or in {#मंँहिष्ठ, वाजंँ सनिषत्, इन्द्रंँ#}
<div n="lb">{#स्तवाम#} and similar instances in which the long nasal vowel
<div n="lb">does not represent more than the value of a short vowel
<div n="lb">and a nasal, or in which the short nasal vowel is not fol-
<div n="lb">lowed by a final sibilant.) E. {#अनु#} and {#नासिका#}.

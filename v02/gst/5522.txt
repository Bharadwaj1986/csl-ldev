{#अप्रमा#}¦ Tatpur. f. ({#-मा#}) The reverse of {#प्रमा,#} viz. <sup>1</sup> The same
as {#अप्रमाण#} I. q. v.; e. g. in {%Mādhava'%}s {%Jaiminīya-nyāyam.:%}
{#सदाचारोऽप्रमा मा वा निर्मूलत्वादमानता । अष्टकादेरिवै-#}
{#तस्य समूलत्वात्प्रमाणता ॥ होलाकोत्सवादिसदाचारस्य मूलभू-#}
{#तवेदाभावादप्रामाण्यमिति चेत् ॥ न#} &c.; or {#गुणनामत्वसंदे-#}
{#हादप्रमा चोदनेति चेत् । नोक्तन्यायेन संघस्य नामधेयत्वनिर्ण-#}
{#यात्#}. <sup>2</sup> (In the Vaiśeṣika philosophy.) Knowledge which
is not absolute, viz. knowledge which is either at variance
with the properties of the thing supposed to be known (see
{#विपर्यास#}), as if one believes, he knows ‘Soul or Self when
he attributes to it properties belonging to Body’, or know-
ledge based on doubt (see {#संशय#}), as if one does not know
whether ‘an object is a man or a post’. E. {#अ#} neg. and {#प्रमा#}.

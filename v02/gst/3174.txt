{#अनुभाव#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-वः#}) <sup>1</sup> Dignity, authority, power (such
<div n="lb">as results from wealth, a magisterial position &c.). <sup>2</sup> Cer-
<div n="lb">tainty, resolution (only in the positive sense of a good re-
<div n="lb">solution, e. g. {#महानुभाव#} q. v.). <sup>3</sup> (In rhetorical terminology.)
<div n="lb">‘A symptom which indicated the feeling ({#भाव#}) produced
<div n="lb">by its appropriate causes’. These causes being naturally
<div n="lb">various from the character ({#रस#}) of a poetical composition,
<div n="lb">the rhetorical works enumerate the anubhāvas which are
<div n="lb">the concomitants of the different sorts of rasas; thus the
<div n="lb">symptoms of the Erotic ({#शृङ्गार#}) are according to them,
<div n="lb">motion of the eye-brows, side-glances &c.; of the Comic
<div n="lb">({#हास्य#}), blinking with the eye, smiles &c.; of the Pathetic
<div n="lb">({#करुण#}), cursing one's fate, falling to the ground, crying
<div n="lb">&c.; of the Terrible ({#रौद्र#}), abuse, fierce looks &c.; of the
<div n="lb">Heroic ({#वीर#}), looking for a companion &c.; of the Fearful
<div n="lb">({#भयानक#}), change of colour, stammering &c.; of the Dis-
<div n="lb">gustful ({#बीभत्स#}), spitting, contracting the mouth, shutting
<div n="lb">the eyes &c.; of the Wonderful ({#अद्भुत#}), wide opening of
<div n="lb">the eyes &c.; of the Quietistic ({#शान्त#}), horripilation (sic)
<div n="lb">&c.; of the Affectionate ({#वत्सल#}), horripilation, joy, tears
<div n="lb">&c.--Compare {#भाव#} and {#विभाव#}. E. {#अनु#} and {#भाव,#} lit.
<div n="lb">‘following or connected with condition, feeling &c.’.

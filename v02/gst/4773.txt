{#अपरान्त#}¦ Karmadh. m. ({#-न्तः#}) The western extremity, the
western border, the western sea-shore; e. g. {#सोऽपरान्तेषु#}
{#तीर्थानि पुण्यान्यायतनानि च । तानि सर्वानि गत्वा स प्रभास-#}
{#मुपजग्मिवान्#} ({%Arjunam.%} {#अपरान्तेषु = समुद्रसमीपेषु पश्चिमेषु;#}
{%Nīlak.%} = {#पश्चिमसमुद्रतीरेषु#}); or {#अपरान्तादहं कृष्ण संप्रती-#}
{#हागतः#} ({%Rāmānanda:%} {#अपरान्तात् = पश्चिमसमुद्रतीरात्#}).--
The plur. {#अपरान्ताः#} is the name of the country or the in-
habitants of the western borders.--{%Wilson%} ({%Viṣṇup.%} p.
189, n. 60) renders {#अपरान्ताः#} ‘the people not beyond the
borders’, as opposed to {#परान्ताः#} ‘the people beyond the
borders’ (i. e. of {#परा#} and {#अन्त#}); but as {#पर#} when opposed to
{#अपर#} assumes meanings corresponding with those of {#पूर्व#} (see
s. v. {#अपर#} II.), {#परान्ताः,#} as contradistinguished from {#अप-#}
{#रान्ताः,#} may mean perhaps ‘the people of the eastern borders’
when {#अपरान्ताः#} would retain the sense given before. See
the following.--<sup>2</sup> Last term, completion, e. g. in the {%Yoga-%}
{%Sūtra:%} {#क्षणप्रतियोगी परिणामोऽपरान्तनिर्ग्राह्यः क्रमः#} ‘ma-
turity (or change) is the reverse of what is but a moment;
by the completion (of maturity) the order (of the moments)
becomes comprehensible’. <sup>3</sup> Death. See the Yoga term {#अ-#}
{#परान्तज्ञान#} and comp. {#पूर्वान्त#} and {#परान्त#}. E. {#अपर#} and {#अन्त#}.

{#अपच#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-चः#}) <sup>1</sup> A man who, from his mode of liv-
ing, does not cook for himself; see the following; e. g.
{#अपचः परिव्राजक;#} or {#अपचो दीक्षितः;#} or a householder in
a state of destitution: {#‘गृहस्थधर्मवृत्तो यो ददातिपरिवर्जितः ।#}
{#ऋषिभिर्धर्मतत्त्वज्ञैरपचः स प्रकीर्तितः’;#} eating the food of
such a man, entails on the receiver the penance of the
Chāndrāyaṇa {#‘अपचस्य च भुक्त्वान्नं द्विजश्चान्द्रायणं चरेत्’#}.
<sup>2</sup> One who cannot cook. <sup>3</sup> A bad cook. <sup>4</sup> Different from
one who cooks. (In the second and third meaning the word
[Page159-b+ 56]
is udātta on the last syllable; in the other meanings it is
udātta on the first syllable.) E. {#अ#} neg. or deter. and {#पच#}.

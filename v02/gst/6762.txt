{#अभ्याश#}¦ Tatpur. 1. m. f. n. ({#-शः-शा-शम्#}) Near, proximate;
<div n="lb">e. g. {%Kumāras.:%} {#तया व्याहृतसंदेशा सा बभौ निभृता प्रिये ।#}
<div n="lb">{#चूतयष्टिरिवाभ्याशे मधौ#} (in the approaching spring) {#परभृ-#}
<div n="lb">{#तोन्मुखी;#} or {%Patanj.%} on {%Pāṇ.%} V. 4. 50 v. 3: {#समीपीभवति ।#}
<div n="lb">{#अभ्याशीभवति । अन्तिकीभवति#} &c.; comp. {#समभ्याशीकरण;#}
<div n="lb">a noun depending on it stands in the gen. or abl.; e. g.
<div n="lb">{%Kāś.%} on {%Pāṇ.%} II. 3. 34.: {#अभ्याशं ग्रामस्य#} or {#ग्रामात्#}.
<div n="P">2. n. ({#-शम्#}) Neighbourhood, vicinity; e. g. {%Mahābh.%}
<div n="lb">{%Draupadīhar.:%} {#धनुषोऽभ्याशमागत्य तस्थौ गिरिरिवाचलः;#}
<div n="lb">or {%Nalop.:%} {#सहसाभ्यागतां भैमीमभ्याशपरिवर्तिनीम् । जग्राहा-#}
<div n="lb">{#जगरः#} &c.--It retains its abl. ending when compounded
<div n="lb">with a past partic. in {#क्त;#} e. g. {%Kāś.:%} {#अभ्याशादागतः#}. [The
<div n="lb">{%Amarak.%} and {%Hem.%} give the word as having three genders;
<div n="lb">the {%Med.%} and {%Trikāṇḍaś.%} have the form {#अभ्यास#} as a masc.
<div n="lb">in the sense of {%vicinity;%} on the latter form see the remark
<div n="lb">at the end. A masc. nom. {#अभ्याशः#} in the sense ‘neigh-
<div n="lb">bourhood’, has not come under my observation; the cases
<div n="lb">{#°शस्य, °शम्#} &c. are better referred to a neuter.]
<div n="P">3. m. ({#-शः#}) Result, consequence; e. g. {%Chhānd. Up.:%}
<div n="lb">{#स य एतदेवं विद्वान्साधु सामेत्युपास्तेऽभ्याशो ह यदेनं साधवो#}
<div n="lb">{#धर्मा आ च गच्छेयुरुप च नमेयुः#} ({%Śaṅk.:%} {#साधुगुणवद्विद्वांस्त-#}
<div n="lb">{#स्यैतत्फलमभ्याशः#} &c.); or {#तद्य इह रमणीयचरणा अभ्याशो#}
<div n="lb">{#ह यत्ते रमणीयां योनिमापद्येरन्#} ({%Śaṅk.:%} {#तेनानुशयेन पुण्येन#}
<div n="lb">{#कर्मणा चन्द्रमण्डले भुक्तशेषेणाभ्याशो ह#} &c. (In other pas-
<div n="lb">sages of {%Dr. Röer'%}s ed. of the {%Chh. Up.%} the word is written,
<div n="lb">when implying the same sense, {#अभ्यास,#} e. g. {#कामं ध्याय-#}
<div n="lb">{#न्नप्रमत्तोऽभ्यासो ह यदस्मै स कामः समृध्येत;#} or {#स य एतमेवं#}
<div n="lb">{#विद्वानादित्यं ब्रह्मेत्युपास्तेऽभ्यासो ह यदेनं साधवो घोषा आ#}
<div n="lb">{#च गच्छेयुः#} &c.) E. {#अश्#} with {#अभि,#} kṛt aff. {#घञ्#}. [The
<div n="lb">comm. to the {%Amarak.%} who give only the sense ‘near’,
<div n="lb">allow the word to be written {#अभ्याश#} or {#अभ्यास,#} equally
<div n="lb">so {%Vallabhag.%} on {%Hem.--Rāyamukuṭa%} is even at hand
<div n="lb">with an E. {#सद्#} with {#आ#} and {#अभि,#} kṛt aff. {#ड,#} which he
<div n="lb">founds on the {%Vārtt.%} to {%Pāṇ.%} III. 2. 101.; or {#अस्#} (cl. 4)
<div n="lb">with {#अभि,#} kṛt aff. {#घञ्#}. But though the confusion be-
<div n="lb">tween the two forms is undeniable, it is certain that the
<div n="lb">best Mss., and among them the grammatical ones, write
<div n="lb">always {#अभ्याश#} in the sense of ‘near’; and it seems to me,
<div n="lb">too, that the meaning ‘result’ is better referred to {#अभ्याश,#}
<div n="lb">than to {#अभ्यास,#} because it follows more naturally, like
<div n="lb">that of ‘near’, from a radical expressing quickness, per-
<div n="lb">vasion, than from the radical {#अस्,#} ‘to throw’. An ana-
<div n="lb">logy for both meanings is afforded by {#अभ्यागम#} q. v.]

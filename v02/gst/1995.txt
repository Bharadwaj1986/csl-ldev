<L>1995<pc>058-b<k1>अधोक्षज<k2>अधोऽक्षज
{#अधोऽक्षज#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-जः#}) <sup>1</sup> A name of Kṛṣṇa. <sup>2</sup> The sign
Śravaṇā. Also written {#अधोक्षज#}. E. {#अधस्-अक्ष#} and {#ज;#}
‘Kṛṣṇa, as a child, killed, when lying under a car, Pūtanā,
[Page059-a+ 56]
a female fiend who was hovering over it in the shape of a
bird, and thus, as it were, was born again (under the car)’;
or according to others, from {#अधोक्ष,#} the name of a Ṛṣi,
and {#ज,#} ‘a son of Adhoksha’; or, ‘because he is produced by
or to such as have subdued or cast down their passions,
i. e. because he becomes visible to them’; or a Bahuvr. from
{#अधस्#} and {#अक्षज#} (n. knowledge derived from the senses;
m. pl. living beings), ‘because he has subdued all untrue
knowledge or because he is superior to all living beings’.

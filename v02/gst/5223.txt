{#अपुत्रिका#}¦ I. Tatpur. f. ({#-का#}) A daughter who is not {#पुत्रिका#}
<div n="lb">i. e. who is not appointed by her father, on failure of a
<div n="lb">son, to raise up male issue for him, for the purpose of
<div n="lb">performing his obsequies; e. g. {#पुत्रिकौरसयोः समो धना-#}
<div n="lb">{#धिकारः । अपुत्रिकायास्तूढायाः पुत्रादिन्यूनोपकारकस्वपुत्रद्वा-#}
<div n="lb">{#रेणोपकारकत्वम्#}. (Not to be confounded with the Bahuvr.
<div n="lb">{#अपुत्रिका,#} fem. of {#अपुत्रक#} q. v.) E. {#अ#} neg. and {#पुत्रिका#}.
<div n="P">II. Bahuvr. m. ({#-कः#}) A father who has left no daughter
<div n="lb">appointed by him to be a {#पुत्रिका#} q. v.--{%Jīmūtavāhana%} ex-
<div n="lb">plains in a verse of {%Devala%} which treats of the succession
<div n="lb">of daughters when the father leaves no widow: {#कन्याभ्यश्च#}
<div n="lb">{#पितुर्द्रव्याद्देयं वैवाहिकं वसु । अपुत्रिकस्य कन्या स्वा धर्मजा#}
<div n="lb">{#पुत्रवद्भवेत्,#} the word {#पुत्रिका#} as {%implying%} the meaning ‘son’:
<div n="lb">{#पुत्रिकापदं पुत्रोपलक्षणम्#} when the verse would have to be
<div n="lb">rendered: ‘to unmarried daughters a nuptial portion must
<div n="lb">be given out of the estate of the father; of a father who
<div n="lb">has no {#पुत्रिका#} in the place of a son, his own daughter (i. e.
<div n="lb">one of the same caste) lawfully begotten must be considered
<div n="lb">like a son (i. e. be entitled to the inheritance); the same
<div n="lb">sense is conveyed by {%Parāśara'%}s words: {#अपुत्रस्य मृतस्य कु-#}
<div n="lb">{#मारी रिक्थं गृह्लीयात्#} ({%Dāyabh.%} p. 271, {%Dāyakramas.%} p. 4,
<div n="lb">{%Vīramitrod.%} p. 204 a. with the v. 1. {#अपुत्रमृतस्य#}), and by the
<div n="lb">quotation of the {%Dāyakramas.%} p. 4: {#अपुत्रस्य च स्वा कन्या#}
<div n="lb">{#धर्मजा पुत्रवद्धरेत्#} (which seems to have been the reading
<div n="lb">of {%Colebrooke%} when he translates in his Digest vol. III. p. 491.
<div n="lb">CCCCXX: ‘and his own daughter…shall {%take%} &c.’. But
<div n="lb">the more precise bearing of {%Devala'%}s clause is conveyed by
<div n="lb">a verse of {%Vṛhaspati%} which is to the same effect, viz. {#स-#}
<div n="lb">{#दृशी सदृशेनोढा साध्वी शुश्रूषणे रता । कृताकृता वापुत्रस्य#}
<div n="lb">{#पितुर्धनहरी तु सा#} ({%Dāyabh.%} p. 273, {%Dāyakramas.%} p. 4, {%Vīra-%}
[Page196-b+ 56]
<div n="lb">{%mitr.%} p. 204{%b, Colebr. Dig.%} III. p. 186. CCXXIV. 2.); for he in-
<div n="lb">troduces there the terms {#कृता#} and {#अकृता#} which apply only
<div n="lb">to a {#पुत्रिका#} either appointed by a plain declaration or by
<div n="lb">an implied intention ({%Manu%} 9. 136., {%Dig.%} III. 164. CCVII) and
<div n="lb">the author of the {%Smṛtichandrikā%} (accord. to the {%Vīramitrod.%}
<div n="lb">p. 205 {%a%}) infers from this verse that the {#पुत्रिका,#} whether
<div n="lb">{#कृता#} or {#अकृता,#} precedes in succession the wife of the de-
<div n="lb">ceased, who herself precedes the daughter qualified by the four
<div n="lb">epithets {#सदृशी…रता,#} viz. {#सदृशी सदृशेनोढेति प्राक्पठितं#}
<div n="lb">{#वृहस्पतिवचनं स्मृतिचन्द्रिकाकार इत्थं व्याचख्यौ । अत्राद्यानि#}
<div n="lb">{#चत्वारि विशेषणानि पत्न्या ऊर्ध्वमर्थग्राहिणीदुहितृविषयाणि ।#}
<div n="lb">{#ततः प्राग्धनग्राहिणीदुहितृविशेषणे द्वे अवशिष्टे । कृताकृता वे-#}
<div n="lb">{#त्यत्र पुत्रिकेति विशेष्याध्याहारः । इतरत्र दुहितेति विशेष्या-#}
<div n="lb">{#ध्याहारः । वाशब्दश्च व्यवस्थितविकल्पार्थः । एवं चायमर्थः ।#}
<div n="lb">{#औरसपुत्रविहीनस्य पितुर्धनं द्विविधापि पुत्रिका पत्न्याः पूर्वं#}
<div n="lb">{#गृह्लीयात् । सवर्णादिविशेषणोपेता तु दुहिता तत ऊर्ध्वमिति#}.
<div n="lb">Now as the regular line of succession is in the first rank
<div n="lb">a son, then on failure of him the widow, and on failure
<div n="lb">of her the daughter &c. (comp. {%Dig.%} III. p. 489. CCCCXVII)
<div n="lb">and as a {#पुत्रिका#} is equal in right to a son ({%Kullūka%} on
<div n="lb">{%Manu%} 9. 130. and comp. 9. 127.), the word {#अपुत्रिकस्य#} of {%De-%}
<div n="lb">{%vala,%} it is true, coincides in legal value with the word {#अ-#}
<div n="lb">{#पुत्रस्य#} of {%Parāśara%} &c., but on the other hand it is clear
<div n="lb">that the former is a more precise expression than the latter,
<div n="lb">since an {#अपुत्रिक#} will always be an {#अपुत्र,#} but an {#अपुत्र#}
<div n="lb">may not be an {#अपुत्रिक#}. (The Pandit who has supplied the
<div n="lb">notes to Colebrooke's Digest denies the necessity of the
<div n="lb">former part of this inference (vol. III. p. 188), for a father
<div n="lb">may, in his opinion, appoint his daughter even if he has a
<div n="lb">son, yet his argument is not founded on the law-authorities,
<div n="lb">but on a precedent mentioned in the {%Bhāgav. Pur.%} (4. 1. 2.)
<div n="lb">where Manu appoints his daughter Ākūti as {#पुत्रिका,#} when
<div n="lb">giving her in marriage to Ruchi, although she had brothers.)
<div n="lb">{%Jīmūtavāhana'%}s gloss is therefore not to be rendered ‘{#पुत्रिका#}
<div n="lb">{%means%}’, but ‘{#पुत्रिका#} {%implies%} the meaning son’, when the
<div n="lb">concordance between the quoted authorities becomes com-
<div n="lb">plete. As {%Colebrooke%} has probably translated the reading
<div n="lb">{#अपुत्रस्य च स्वा कन्या#} &c., mentioned before, he has no re-
<div n="lb">mark on the difficulty conveyed by the reading {#अपुत्रिकस्य#}
<div n="lb">(but comp. vol. III. p. 493. 1. 38 ff.).--[A conjecture has
<div n="lb">been proposed to read {#अपुत्रकस्य#} instead of the latter
<div n="lb">word; but as it is advanced without any argument what-
<div n="lb">ever, and is in the teeth of {%Jīmūtavāhana'%}s gloss which is
<div n="lb">misunderstood in rendering {#अपुत्रिक#} ‘sonless’, it seems
<div n="lb">merely to proceed from a gratuitous trifling with the text.]

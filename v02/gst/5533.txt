{#अप्रमुदित#}¦ Tatpur. 1. m. f. n. ({#-तः-ता-तम्#}) Not delighted,
<div n="lb">not happy.
<div n="P">2. n. ({#-तम्#}) (In the Sāṅkhya philosophy.) The reverse
<div n="lb">of {#प्रमुदित#} q. v., a technical term to denote the incapacity
<div n="lb">of removing the pains that are produced by any created
<div n="lb">beings viviparous, oviparous, generated by heat and moisture
<div n="lb">or springing from the soil (as men, beasts, tame and wild,
<div n="lb">birds, reptiles, gnats, musquitoes, lice, bugs, fish, alliga-
<div n="lb">tors, trees, stones &c.), by not attending for this purpose
<div n="lb">a holy teacher (the removal of such pains being required
<div n="lb">for the attainment of final liberation); it is amongst the
<div n="lb">seventeen {#बुद्धिबध#} or obstructions of intellect one of the
<div n="lb">eight which are the converse of the {#सिद्धि#} or perfections.
<div n="lb">For the other terms of this category see {#अतार#} (Supple-
<div n="lb">ment), {#अतारतार#} (Supplement), {#अप्रमोद, अप्रमोदमान,#}
<div n="lb">{#अरम्यक, असदाप्रमुदित, असुतार#}. E. {#अ#} neg. and {#प्रमुदित#}.

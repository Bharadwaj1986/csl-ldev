<div n="P">II. Bahuvr. m. ({#-यः#}) <sup>1</sup> (In the Nyāya philosophy.)
<div n="lb">Founded on the mutual relation (between two objects),
<div n="lb">one of the five modes of a {#तर्क#} (q. v.), viz. an {%argumen-%}
<div n="lb">{%tatio ad absurdum,%} which consists in founding a premise
<div n="lb">on the imaginary relation between two objects and in
<div n="lb">drawing a conclusion from it which is inadmissible.
<div n="lb">This kind of argumentation may again ‘be threefold ac-
<div n="lb">cording to whether the argument of the premise is taken
<div n="lb">from the notion of origin or from that of actuality or from
<div n="lb">that of comprehension’ ({#‘स चोत्पत्तिस्थितिज्ञप्तिद्वारा त्रेधा#}
<div n="lb">{#…। तदपेक्षापेक्षित्वनिबन्धनोऽनिष्टप्रसङ्गः ॥’#}). For
<div n="lb">the other four modes of a {#तर्क#} see: {#आत्माश्रय, चक्रक, अन-#}
<div n="lb">{#वस्था#} and {#तदन्यबाधितार्थप्रसङ्ग#}. [A commentator of the
<div n="lb">Sāṅkhya-Pravachana applies three of these terms to the ex-
<div n="lb">planation of the Sūtra: {#‘तद्योगे तत्सिद्धावन्योन्याश्रयत्वम्’#} in

<div n="lb">{#त्माश्रयत्वमनवस्था वेति शेषः ॥’#}.] <sup>2</sup> (In the Sāṅkhya philos.)
<div n="lb">Resting on one another, supporting one another; a property

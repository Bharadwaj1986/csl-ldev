{#अभिनिधान#}¦ Tatpur. n. ({#-नम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Placing over, putting over;
<div n="lb">e. g. {%Kātyāy. Śrautas.:%} {#आयुरसीत्युत्तरयाज्यस्थालीं संस्पृश्य पु-#}
<div n="lb">{#रूरवा इत्यभिनिधानं तया;#} ({%comm.%} {#उत्तरारण्या अधरारणे-#}
<div n="lb">{#रुपरि निधानं करोति#}). <sup>2</sup> (In the {%Prātiśakhyas%}) Suppression
<div n="lb">or weakening of a sound with regard to, or on account
<div n="lb">of, scil. the influence of a preceding or following sound
<div n="lb">[the qualification {%‘with regard to’%} or {%‘on account of’%} seems
<div n="lb">to be implied by the prefix {#अभि#}], viz. <sup>a.</sup> the suppression
<div n="lb">of the vowel {#अ#} through the influence of a {%preceding%} {#ओ#} or
<div n="lb">{#ए#} (comp. {#अभिनिहित#}); e. g. in {#गाहमानोऽदयः#} of the word
<div n="lb">{#अदयः,#} or in {#वालेऽवत#} of the word {#अवत;#} <sup>b.</sup> the sup-
<div n="lb">pression or weakening of the sound or of the vibration of
<div n="lb">the voice which naturally follows the pronunciation of a
<div n="lb">consonant, if such a consonant is {%followed%} by, and has made
<div n="lb">{%sandhi%} with, another consonant; according to the {%Ṛgv.%}
<div n="lb">{%Prātiś.,%} such a sound or the vibration of the voice is {%sup-%}
<div n="lb">{%pressed,%} if the consonant is surd ({#क, च#} &c.); it is {%weakened,%}
<div n="lb">if the consonant is sonant ({#ग, ज#} &c.) and the sound which
<div n="lb">{%remains,%} after such weakening is called {#ध्रुव#} (q. v.). This
<div n="lb">suppression or weakening of the vibration of the voice,
<div n="lb">however, takes place only under the following restrictions:
<div n="lb">1. in the pronunciation of a {#स्पर्श#} or {#अन्तःस्था#} (except {#र्#}),
[Page248-a+ 56]
<div n="lb">if it is followed by a {#स्पर्श;#} e. g. in {#अर्वाग्देवः#} or in {#उल्काम्;#}
<div n="lb">2. of a {#स्पर्श,#} if it stands in a pause; e. g. in {#वाक्;#} 3. of an
<div n="lb">{#अन्तःस्था,#} if it is followed by another similar {#अन्तःस्था;#}
<div n="lb">e. g. in {#तव्वंँः;#} 4 of {#ल्,#} if it is followed by an {#ऊष्मन्;#} e. g.
<div n="lb">in {#शतवल्शः;#} 5. of {#क्#} in {#अक्ख्यत्;#} 6. of {#प्#} in {#विरप्सी;#} 7. of
<div n="lb">a {#स्पर्श#} except {#म्#} (i. e. {#क्#} till {#भ्#}), if it is followed by {#य्,#}
<div n="lb">{#र्, व्#} or an {#ऊष्मन्;#} e. g. in {#यद्यत्, तद्रासभः, यान्वः, अर्वा-#}
<div n="lb">{#क्शफौ;#} (the latter cases 4-7. only in the theory of the {%Śā-%}
<div n="lb">{%kalas%}). These general rules are subject to exceptions and
<div n="lb">restrictions, and even as such they are not universally re-
<div n="lb">cognized; {%Śaunaka,%} for instance, says, that {%Vyāĺi%} does not

<div n="lb">when the following consonant is doubled or if a vowel or
<div n="lb">{#र्#} precede.--The {%Vājas. Prātiś.%} uses instead of the term
<div n="lb">{#अभिनिधान#} the explanation {#स्थानकरणविमोक्षः#} ‘giving up
<div n="lb">the characteristics of a consonant, viz. the organic dis-
<div n="lb">tinction and the distinction with regard to the effort of pro-

<div n="lb">{%Stud.%} IV. p. 127), but applies it merely to the {#स्पर्श#} at the
<div n="lb">end of a word, if it is followed by another word, and in

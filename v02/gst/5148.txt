<L>5148<pc>189-b<k1>अपाश्रय<k2>अपाश्रय
{#अपाश्रय#}¦ I. Tatpur. m. ({#-यः#}) <sup>1</sup> A support, a recipient, the
person on which another reposes, as it were, by whom he
is protected; e. g. {#ब्राह्मणापाश्रयो नित्यमुत्कृष्टां जातिमश्नुते#}
(scil. {#शूद्रः#}) ‘a Śūdra who is supported by a Brahmaṇa
obtains always a superior birth’; (speculation has been rife
on this passage of {%Manu%} 9. 335.: both Calc. editions read
{#ब्राह्मणायाश्रयो#}--which is meaningless--, another reading
is {#ब्राह्मणोपाश्रयो,#} and the correctness of the word {#अपाश्रय#}
in this verse has been even doubted; but it occurs and is
commented upon in the same sense ‘recipient, support’ e. g.
in the {%Bhāgavata Pur.%} VI. 19. 12. where Viṣṇu is contrasted
with Śrī:) {#त्वं हि सर्वशरीर्यात्मा श्रीः शरीरेन्द्रियाशयाः । ना-#}
{#मरूपे भगवती प्रत्ययस्त्वमपाश्रयः#} (comm. {#प्रत्ययो नामरूपयोः#}
{#प्रकाशः । अपाश्रयः । तयोराधारः#}); i. e. ‘…thou
makest them manifest and thou art their recipient or sup-
port’.--Comp. also {#अपाश्रित#}. <sup>2</sup> That which rests upon; (used
in this sense {%Daśakumārach.%} ed. {%Wilson%} p. 90, 1. 13, where the
word implies the meaning ‘head’ as resting on the hand).
<sup>3</sup> An awning spread over a court or yard. E. {#श्रि#} with {#आ#}
and {#अप,#} kṛt aff. {#अच्#}.
<div n="P">II. Bahuvr. m. f. n. ({#-यः-या-यम्#}) Unsupported, helpless.
E. {#अप#} and {#आश्रय#}.

{#अभोक्तृत्व#}¦ Tatpur. n. ({#-त्वम्#}) The condition of one who does
not enjoy; in the {%Sāṅkhya phil.%} the condition of {%Prakṛti%}
or Matter in contradistinction from that of {%Purusha%} or Soul,
for whose benefit or enjoyment the former is created; e. g.
[Page304-b+ 56]
{%Sāṅkhya Prav.:%} {#प्रधानसृष्टिः परार्थं स्वतोऽप्यभोक्तृत्वादुष्ट्र-#}
{#कुङ्कुमवहनवत्#} ‘Matter is created on account of some one
other; for though its creation is spontaneous, it cannot
enjoy (being unintelligent), like as a camel carries saffron
(for some one else, i. e. for its master)’. E. {#अ#} neg. and
{#भोक्तृत्व#}.

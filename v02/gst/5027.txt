{#अपहार#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-रः#}) <sup>1</sup> Taking away, carrying off, re-
<div n="lb">moving; also figuratively; comp. {#आत्मापहार#}. <sup>2</sup> (In Law.)
<div n="lb"><sup>a.</sup> Stealing, robbing; see {#अपहरण#} and the definition given
<div n="lb">of the latter word. <sup>b.</sup> Concealing, secreting; according to
<div n="lb">{%Jīmūtavāhana%} in the passage of the {%Dāyabhāga:%} {#न साधारण-#}
<div n="lb">{#धनापहारे स्तेयनिष्पत्तिः#} ‘the notion of theft is not completed

<div n="lb">more)’, when the word {#अपहार#} is thus explained: {#अपहा-#}
<div n="lb">{#रपदं तु संगोपनाभिप्रायम् । न च संगोपनं स्तेयमुक्तम् । असं-#}
<div n="lb">{#गुप्तहरणेऽपि स्तेयपदप्रदर्शनात्#}. <sup>c.</sup> Withholding property
<div n="lb">from its lawful owner; in the passage of the {%Dāyabhāga%}
<div n="lb">and the {%Dāyakramasaṅgraha:%} {#स्त्रीणां स्वपतिदायस्तु उपभो-#}
<div n="lb">{#गफलः स्मृतः । नापहारं स्त्रियः कुर्युः पतिदायात्कथंचन;#} where
<div n="lb">{%Jīmūtavāhana%} explains {#अपहार#} in this manner: {#अपहारश्च#}
<div n="lb">{#धनस्वाम्यनुपयोगे भवति#}. (The quotation from the {%Dāyabhāga%}

<div n="lb"><sup>4</sup> (In Astronomy.) The configuration of the minor planets

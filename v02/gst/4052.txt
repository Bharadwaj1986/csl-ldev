<L>4052<pc>132-a<k1>अन्धकारप्रायश्चित्त<k2>अन्धकारप्रायश्चित्त
{#अन्धकारप्रायश्चित्त#}¦ Tatpur. n. ({#-त्तम्#}) A penance mentioned in
<div n="lb">the Varāha-Purāṇa, to atone for the offence of approaching,
<div n="lb">in the dark, the image of Viṣṇu in his incarnation as a
<div n="lb">boar, without a lamp and without the Śāstra; such an offence
<div n="lb">degrading the offender and exposing him to becoming blind:
<div n="lb">the penance consists in veiling the eyes during fifteen days,
<div n="lb">taking only one meal on the following twentieth day and
<div n="lb">one meal on each twelfth day of the half of a month, and
<div n="lb">when the penitent is half dead in consequence of such diet,
<div n="lb">sitting in the water and ultimately eating a dish of barley
<div n="lb">and rice cooked in cow's urine. He is promised to be rid
<div n="lb">then of his crime. Another reading of this word is {#अन्धका-#}
<div n="lb">{#रस्पृष्टप्रायश्चित्त#}. E. {#अन्धकार#} and {#प्रायश्चित्त#}.

{#अन्तःकरण#}¦ Tatpur. n. ({#-णम्#}) (In Philosophy.) <sup>1</sup> The interior
<div n="lb">sense (also called {#आभ्यन्तरकरण#}) opposed to the {#वाह्यकरण#}
<div n="lb">(q. v.) or exterior sense; this notion which in general may
<div n="lb">be termed {%‘the faculty of thinking’%} is thus modified in the
<div n="lb">Vedānta and in the Sāṅkhya: <sup>a</sup> (In the Vedānta it implies)
<div n="lb">the notion of what is the general substratum of {#आत्मन्#} or
<div n="lb">Soul (Śaṅkara: {#तच्चात्मन उपाधिभूतमन्तःकरणम्#}); its activity
<div n="lb">becomes manifest in two of the seventeen {#सूक्ष्मशरीर#} (q. v.)
<div n="lb">or subtle bodies of creation, viz. in the {#बुद्धि#} (q. v.) or that
<div n="lb">mental activity which forms positive conclusions, and in
<div n="lb">the {#मनस्,#} or that mental activity which ‘discerns and doubts’;
[Page115-a+ 56]
<div n="lb">and as {#चित्त#} or ‘logical combination’ is involved by the
<div n="lb">activity of {#बुद्धि,#} and {#अहङ्कार#} or ‘self-consciousness’ is in-
<div n="lb">volved by {#मनस्,#} the activity of the {#अन्तःकरण#} is fourfold,
<div n="lb">viz. as that of {#बुद्धि, मनस्, चित्त#} and {#अहङ्कार#}. Śaṅkara in
<div n="lb">his commentary on the Vedānta Sūtras varies slightly in this
<div n="lb">respect by substituting {#विज्ञान#} for {#अहङ्कार;#} the difference
<div n="lb">however is merely apparent, {#विज्ञान,#} worldly knowledge,
<div n="lb">being also one of the attributes of {#मनस्#} (Śaṅkara: {#‘अन्तः-#}
<div n="lb">{#करणं मनो बुद्धिर्विज्ञानं चित्तमिति चानेकधा तत्र तत्राभि-#}
<div n="lb">{#लप्यते’#}). <sup>b.</sup> (In the Sāṅkhya it is not the substratum of {#पुरुष#}
<div n="lb">or Soul but) the generic name and characteristic of three
<div n="lb">successive productions of {#प्रकृति#} or Matter, viz. of {#बुद्धि#} ‘as-
<div n="lb">certainment or positive conclusion’, of its product {#अहङ्कार#}
<div n="lb">self-consciousness’ and of the product of Ahaṅkāra, viz.
<div n="lb">{#मनस्#} which in this philosophy means ‘an organ both of sen-
<div n="lb">sation and of action’ (see {#बुद्धीन्द्रिय#} and {#कर्मेन्द्रिय#}), ‘the
<div n="lb">organ of volition’ (see {#सङ्कल्प#}). ‘The ten external organs
<div n="lb">(viz the five organs of sensation and the five of action)
<div n="lb">supply the objects for the ‘interior sense’, their time is the
<div n="lb">present time, while the interior sense embraces the past,
<div n="lb">present and future time’. <sup>2</sup> (In the non-philosophical lite-
<div n="lb">rature where philosophical terms are usually employed in
<div n="lb">a vague manner, this word means) Mind, heart, feeling,
<div n="lb">a synonyme of {#मनस्#} in its vague, unphilosophical sense;
<div n="lb">e. g. (in a verse of the Mālatīmādhava where an apparent
<div n="lb">show is made of philosophical accuracy) {#प्रेमार्द्राः प्रणयस्पृ-#}
<div n="lb">{#शः…चेष्टा भवेयुर्मयि । यास्वन्तःकरणस्य वाह्यकरणव्यापा-#}
<div n="lb">{#ररोधीक्षणादाशंसापरिकल्पितास्वपि भवत्यानन्दसान्द्रोदयः#}
<div n="lb">(var. lect. {#°सान्द्रो लयः#})--E. {#अन्तर्#} and {#करण#}.

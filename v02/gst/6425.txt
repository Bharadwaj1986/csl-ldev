{#अभिषव#}¦ Tatpur. 1. m. ({#-वः#}) <sup>1</sup> The religious act of pressing
<div n="lb">out the juice of the Soma plant or Asclepias acida (see
<div n="lb">also {#सोमाभिषव#}), or in default of this plant, of its substi-
<div n="lb">tute, the Pūtīkā or Cæsalpinia bonducella (see also {#पूती-#}
<div n="lb">{#काभिषव#}); it constitutes an essential ceremony of the
<div n="lb">Soma sacrifices (see {#ज्योतिष्टोम#}), and is performed the day
<div n="lb">after the sacrificial acts called {#उपसद्#} (q. v.), by means
<div n="lb">of stones ({#ग्रावन्#}) which press on two boards (called
<div n="lb">{#अधिषवण#}); the act takes place in the southern part of a
<div n="lb">building erected in the western part of the Uttaravedi near
<div n="lb">a cart which is placed there and bears, like the building,
<div n="lb">the name {#हविर्धान#}. (Comp. e. g. {%Mādhava vv. ll.:%} {#यदि#}

<div n="lb">{#वसादूर्ध्वं सोमाभिषवदिवसात्पूर्वं कर्तव्या होमा उपसदः;#}--

{#अनुषङ्ग#}¦ Tatpur. m. ({#-ङ्गः#}) <sup>1</sup> Connexion, conjunction, association,
attachment (lit. and fig.) e. g. {#(वारिणा) अन्तर्गलितेन मन्म-#}
{#थशिखीसिक्तोऽनुषङ्गोद्भवः#} (comm. {#प्रियस्मरणादुत्पन्नः#}).
<sup>2</sup> Tenderness, compassion. <sup>3</sup> Necessary consequence, the
connexion of a subsequent with a previous act. <sup>4</sup> Gram-
matical relation, connexion of, or connecting a word of a
preceding with those of a subsequent passage; e. g. {#कच्चि-#}
{#दित्यस्यानुषङ्गं केचिन्नेच्छन्ति#} as the Schol. observes with re-
ference to {#कच्चित्#} in Bhattik. 6. 67 when commenting on 6. 68,
or in the Mīmānsā: {#अनुषङ्गो वाक्यपरिसमाप्तिः सर्वेषु तुल्य-#}
{#योगित्वात्#}. Comp. {#अनुषञ्जन#}. (Different from {#अध्याहार#}.)
<sup>5</sup> (In Grammar.) Appendage, viz. the nasal which is con-
nected with certain radicals and dropped in certain deriva-
tives; e. g. the nasal in {#तृम्प्, दृम्प्, शुम्भ्, सञ्ज्#} &c. E. {#सञ्ज्#}
with {#अनु,#} kṛt aff. {#अच्#}.

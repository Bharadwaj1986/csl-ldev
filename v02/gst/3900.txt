<L>3900<pc>126-a<k1>अन्तर्वत्<k2>अन्तर्वत्
{#अन्तर्वत्#}¦ 1. m. f. n. ({#-वान्-वती-वत्#}) <sup>1</sup> Occupying the in-
terior, e. g. of a hall, a house, {#घटः#} ‘a jar in the interior
of a hall’; {#अन्तर्वती शाला#} ‘a hall in the interior of another
hall’. <sup>2</sup> Probably the same as {#आत्मवत्#} (comp. the mean-
ing of {#अन्तर्#} IV. 2.), selfpossessed, composed, as in the
vaidik instance {#पुमानन्तर्वान्त्स्थविरः पयस्वान्वसोः कबन्धमृ-#}
{#षभो बिभर्ति;#} (it has been proposed somewhere to translate
{#अन्तर्वत्#} in this instance ‘pregnant’).
<div n="P">2. f. ({#-त्नी;#} in the Vedas also {#-वती#}) A pregnant woman.
[As the instance {#अन्तर्वती शाला#} has been taken from the
Kāśikā on Pāṇ. IV. 1. 32, it is necessary to observe that
Bhaṭṭojidīkṣsta in the Prauḍhamanor. rejects this forma-
tion together with the counter-instance {#पतिवती#} in the same
rule: {#‘यत्तु प्राचान्तर्वती पतिवतीति प्रत्युदाहृतं तदयुक्तम्’#}.]
E. {#अन्तर्,#} taddh. aff. {#मतुप्#} and āgama {#नुक्#}.

<div n="P">2. n. ({#-स्तम्#}) (In {%Pāṇini%}); scil. {#अङ्ग#} q. v. The {%redupli-%}
<div n="lb">{%cated base%} of a radical, in general, in the preterite ({#लिट्#}),
<div n="lb">the aorist ({#चङ्#}), the desider. ({#सन्#}) and the intens. ({#यङ्#})

<div n="lb">--comp. VI. 1. 8. 9. 11.--, moreover the reduplicated base
<div n="lb">of a radical of the third class ({#हु#} &c. VI. 1. 10.) and the
<div n="lb">{%base%} (being in reality a reduplicated one) of {#जक्ष्, जागृ,#}
<div n="lb">{#दरिद्रा, चकास्, दीधी, वेवी#} and {#शास्#} (to which {%Kātyāy.%}
<div n="lb">adds, though not with the consent of {%Patanj.%} and {%Kaiyy.:%}
<div n="lb">{#सस्#} and {#वश्#} VI. 1. 6 and vv.), before a {#सार्वधातुक,#} as well
<div n="lb">as the {%reduplicated base%} of a radical of the named categories
<div n="lb">in the participles (included in the foregoing terms) and in
<div n="lb">such derivatives as {#बभ्रु, ययु, चक्र, चिक्लिद#} &c. (VI. 1. 12 vv).
<div n="lb">The term {#अभ्यस्त#} {%n.%} comprises therefore, in {%Pāṇini,%} the
<div n="lb">radical syllable {%and%} the syllable of reduplication (which
<div n="lb">latter is called by him {#अभ्यास;#} VI. 1. 5.: {#उभे अभ्यस्तम्;#}
<div n="lb">VI. 1. 4.: {#पूर्वोऽभ्यासः#}); e. g. {#ददा#}--, and {#नेनिज्#}--of
<div n="lb">{#ददाति#} and {#नेनिजति#} are {#अभ्यस्तम्#} ({#द#}--and {#ने#}--being
<div n="lb">in these words the {#अभ्यास#}). {%Kātyāyana%} and {%Patanjali%} con-
<div n="lb">clude from this definition of {%Pāṇini%} that the term can only
<div n="lb">be used where there are {%two%} syllables representing the re-
<div n="lb">duplication and that it is not applicable to the first syllable

<div n="lb">{#तत्राभ्यस्तसंज्ञा यथा स्यात्;#} {%Pat.:%} {#इह मा भूत् । ईर्त्सन्ति ईप्स-#}
<div n="lb">{#न्तीति । ईर्त्सन् । ईप्सन् । ऐर्त्सन् । ऐप्सन्#}). [The learned

<div n="lb">comm. of the present ed. of {%Pāṇ.%} have been mistaken, con-
<div n="lb">sequently, when they supply at the term {#अभ्यस्त#} the word

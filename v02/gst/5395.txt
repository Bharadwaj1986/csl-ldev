<L>5395<pc>208-a<k1>अप्रज<k2>अप्रज
{#अप्रज#}¦ Tatpur. 1. m. f. n. ({#-जः-जा-जम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Unborn; e. g. in
the {%Ṛgv.:%} {#अप्रजाः सन्त्वत्रिणः#} ‘may the demons not come
into existence’ ({%Sāyaṇa:%} {#अनुत्पन्नाः सन्तु#}). <sup>2</sup> Not productive
(of children), unprolific; e. g. {#मैथुनम्#}.
<div n="P">2. m. ({#-जः#}) A man who has not begot (children);
e. g. {%Vṛhaspati%} (as quoted by the {%Vīramitrodaya%}): {#सपिण्ड-#}
{#स्याप्रजस्यांशं स्थावरं सप्तधाकृतम्#}.
<div n="P">3. f. ({#-जा#}) <sup>1</sup> A female who has not borne (a child); e. g.
{%Vṛddhakātyāyana%} (as quoted in the {%Dāyat.%}): {#पितृभ्यां चैव#}
{#यद्दत्तं दुहितुः स्थावरं धनम् । अप्रजायामतीतायां भ्रातृगामि#}
{#तु सर्वदा#}. <sup>2</sup> One who cannot bear, a barren female; e. g.
in {%Kumārila'%}s {%Mīmānsā Vārtt.:%} {#प्रजा दश मासान्गर्भं धृत्वा#}
{#एकादशमनु प्रजायन्ते तस्मादश्वतर्योऽप्रजाः#}. E. {#अ#} neg. and
{#प्रज#}. (This word is always {%Tatpur.,%} with the udātta on
the first syllable, and must not be analyzed as a {%Bahuvr.%}
of {#अ#} priv. and {#प्रजा,#} for {%Pāṇini%} enjoins V. 4. 122., that the
latter assumes {%‘always’%} the form {#अप्रजस्,#} and his rule is
neither contradicted nor restricted by any of his commen-
tators; the meanings of {#अप्रज#} and {#अप्रजस्#} will often coin-
cide in bearing--comp. also {#अप्रजाता, अपप्रजाता, अप्र-#}
{#सूता--,#} but their difference will be perceived in such in-
stances as given above, e. g. in {#अश्वतर्योऽप्रजाः#} when the
Bahuvr. epithet ‘without progeny’ would alter the sense,
or in {%Chāṇakya'%}s {#मैथुनमप्रजम्,#} since it would be absurd to
speak of a {#मैथुन#} ‘without children’, instead of an unpro-
lific one.)

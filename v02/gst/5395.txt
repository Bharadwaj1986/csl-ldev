{#अप्रज#}¦ Tatpur. 1. m. f. n. ({#-जः-जा-जम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Unborn; e. g. in
<div n="lb">the {%Ṛgv.:%} {#अप्रजाः सन्त्वत्रिणः#} ‘may the demons not come
<div n="lb">into existence’ ({%Sāyaṇa:%} {#अनुत्पन्नाः सन्तु#}). <sup>2</sup> Not productive
<div n="lb">(of children), unprolific; e. g. {#मैथुनम्#}.
<div n="P">2. m. ({#-जः#}) A man who has not begot (children);
<div n="lb">e. g. {%Vṛhaspati%} (as quoted by the {%Vīramitrodaya%}): {#सपिण्ड-#}
<div n="lb">{#स्याप्रजस्यांशं स्थावरं सप्तधाकृतम्#}.
<div n="P">3. f. ({#-जा#}) <sup>1</sup> A female who has not borne (a child); e. g.
<div n="lb">{%Vṛddhakātyāyana%} (as quoted in the {%Dāyat.%}): {#पितृभ्यां चैव#}
<div n="lb">{#यद्दत्तं दुहितुः स्थावरं धनम् । अप्रजायामतीतायां भ्रातृगामि#}
<div n="lb">{#तु सर्वदा#}. <sup>2</sup> One who cannot bear, a barren female; e. g.
<div n="lb">in {%Kumārila'%}s {%Mīmānsā Vārtt.:%} {#प्रजा दश मासान्गर्भं धृत्वा#}
<div n="lb">{#एकादशमनु प्रजायन्ते तस्मादश्वतर्योऽप्रजाः#}. E. {#अ#} neg. and
<div n="lb">{#प्रज#}. (This word is always {%Tatpur.,%} with the udātta on
<div n="lb">the first syllable, and must not be analyzed as a {%Bahuvr.%}
<div n="lb">of {#अ#} priv. and {#प्रजा,#} for {%Pāṇini%} enjoins V. 4. 122., that the
<div n="lb">latter assumes {%‘always’%} the form {#अप्रजस्,#} and his rule is
<div n="lb">neither contradicted nor restricted by any of his commen-
<div n="lb">tators; the meanings of {#अप्रज#} and {#अप्रजस्#} will often coin-
<div n="lb">cide in bearing--comp. also {#अप्रजाता, अपप्रजाता, अप्र-#}
<div n="lb">{#सूता--,#} but their difference will be perceived in such in-
<div n="lb">stances as given above, e. g. in {#अश्वतर्योऽप्रजाः#} when the
<div n="lb">Bahuvr. epithet ‘without progeny’ would alter the sense,
<div n="lb">or in {%Chāṇakya'%}s {#मैथुनमप्रजम्,#} since it would be absurd to
<div n="lb">speak of a {#मैथुन#} ‘without children’, instead of an unpro-
<div n="lb">lific one.)

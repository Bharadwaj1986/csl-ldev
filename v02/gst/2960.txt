{#अनुत्तर#}¦ I. Tatpur. 1. m. f. n. ({#-रः-रा-रम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Low, nether.
<sup>2</sup> South, southern. <sup>3</sup> Low, inferior, vile.
<div n="P">2. n. ({#-रम्#}) <sup>1</sup> No-answer. <sup>2</sup> A bad or evasive reply
and held therefore to be no-answer (e. g. of the defendant,
in a law-suit). E. {#अ#} neg. and {#उत्तर#}.
<div n="P">II. Bahuvr. 1. m. f. n. ({#-रः-रा-रम्#}) <sup>1</sup> Best, excellent,
supreme, chief, principal (literally: than which there is no
higher or better). <sup>2</sup> Fixed, firm. <sup>3</sup> Unable to answer or to
give a proper answer.
<div n="P">2. m. pl. ({#-राः#}) In Jaina mythology, one of the two
divisions (see also {#ग्रैवेयक#}) of the gods called {#कल्पातीत#}
(q. v.) which are one of the two divisions (see also {#कल्पभव#})
of the gods called {#वैमानिक#} (q. v.); the {#अनुत्तराः#} comprise
five classes. See {#विजय, वैजयन्त, जयन्त, अपराजित#} and
{#सर्वार्थसिद्धि#}. See also {#अनुत्तरोपपातिक#}. E. {#अ#} priv. and {#उत्तर#}.

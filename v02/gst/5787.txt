{#अब्दुर्ग#}¦ Tatpur. n. ({#-र्गम्#}) A water-fortress, i. e. a place sur-
rounded by deep water; {%Manu%} recommends it, amongst
other sites, as one where a king should build his town:
{#धन्वदुर्गं महीदुर्गमब्दुर्गं वार्क्षमेव च । नृदुर्गं गिरिदुर्गं वा स-#}
{#माश्रित्य वसेत्पुरम्#} ({%Kullūka:%} {#जलदुर्गमगाधोदकेन सर्वतः परि-#}
{#वृतम्#}); the best fortress for a king is, however, the {#गि-#}
{#रिदुर्ग#} q. v., for the {#अब्दुर्ग#} is, in the view of Manu, the
appropriate fortress for aquatic beings, the {#धन्वदुर्ग#} q. v.
for wild animals, the {#महीदुर्ग#} q. v. for mice or other ani-
mals living in holes, the {#वृक्षदुर्ग#} q. v. for monkeys, while
the {#नृदुर्ग#} q. v. is the fortress for men and the {#गिरिदुर्ग#}
for gods. E. {#अप्#} and {#दुर्ग#}.

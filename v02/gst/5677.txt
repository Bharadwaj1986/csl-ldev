<L>5677<pc>225-a<k1>अप्सुमत्<k2>अप्सुमत्
{#अप्सुमत्#}¦ m. f. n. ({#-मान्-मती-मत्#}) <sup>1</sup> Having or possessing
<div n="lb">what is in water or arises from water; e. g. Agni so called
<div n="lb">from his combination with the fire of lightning which is in,
<div n="lb">or comes from, the rain-waters; or in the {%Chhāndogya Up.:%}
<div n="lb">{#न हाप्सु प्रैत्यप्सुमान्भवति य एतदेवं विद्वान्सर्वास्वप्सु पञ्चविधं#}
<div n="lb">{#सामोपास्ते#} (where {%Ānandajn.%} explains the word ‘possessed
<div n="lb">with water’, viz. {#असावुपासको मरुस्थलीष्वपि यथेच्छमुदक-#}
<div n="lb">{#वान्भवतीत्यर्थः,#} but it is perhaps better to say that such a
<div n="lb">worshipper becomes possessed with all the benefits that
<div n="lb">are in, or come from, water). <sup>2</sup> Containing, or accom-
<div n="lb">panied with, the word {#अप्सु;#} as verses, e. g. in the {%Śatap.:%}
<div n="lb">{#अप्सुमतीभ्यामग्नेरेतद्वैश्वानरस्य स्तोत्रम्;#} or as oblations, e. g.
<div n="lb">{#अप्सुमन्तावाज्यभागौ,#} whereon the {%Prauḍham.%} observes:
<div n="lb">{#अप्सुमन्ताविति । कारीर्या आज्यभागयोः । अप्सु अग्ने सधिष्ठ#}
<div n="lb">{#च । अप्सु मे सोमोऽब्रवीदित्यनुवाक्ये । तत्र चाप्सुशब्दोऽस्ति#}.
<div n="lb">E. {#अप्सु#} (loc. plur. of {#अप्#}), taddh. aff. {#मतुप्#}.

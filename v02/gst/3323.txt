{#अनुवाक्या#}¦ Tatpur. f. ({#-क्या#}) The name of such verses of the
<div n="lb">Ṛgveda as are recited by the priest Hotṛ (q. v.), when the
<div n="lb">deity is invoked by him to partake of the Havis or clarified
<div n="lb">butter. This word is found frequently together with {#याज्या#}
<div n="lb">(q. v.), the name of those hymns of the same Veda which
<div n="lb">are recited by the Hotṛ when the Havis-oblation is actually
<div n="lb">made. The Anuvākyās are spoken in a slow or as it were
<div n="lb">drawling tone, while the Yājyās are recited quickly. Accord-
<div n="lb">ing to the Mīmānsā no priest but the Hotṛ is allowed to
<div n="lb">recite either of them. Compare also {#पुरोऽनुवाक्या#} and {#या-#}
<div n="lb">{#ज्यानुवाक्या#}. E. {#वच्#} with {#अनु,#} kṛtya aff. {#ण्यत्#}.

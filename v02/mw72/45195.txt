{#वोपदेव#}¦ {%vopa-deva, as,%} m., N. of the author
of the Mugdha-bodha grammar, the Kavi-kalpa-
druma, and various other works (including, according
to some, the Bhāgavata-Purāṇa; he was a son of
Keśava and pupil of Dhaneśvara, and is said to have
flourished about the latter half of the thirteenth
century at the court of Hemādri, king of Deva-giri,
now Dowlatabad).

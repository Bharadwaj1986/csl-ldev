{@Māgandika, (1) = Mākandika@}¦ (Pali Māgandiya):
in the ‘Skt. Suttanipāta’, Hoernle, JRAS 1916.714--5;
({@2@}) as common noun, in Mv iii.412.7 (prose), applied to
some kind of heretical (brahmanical) ascetics or sectarians,
in a cpd., after parivrājakā, traidaṇḍika-mānandika-(so
mss., read māga°, or māka°?)-guruputraka-gautama- etc.);
cf. Pali AN iii.276.32 f., similar list, paribbājako…
māgaṇḍiko…tedaṇḍiko…aviruddhako…gotamako,
etc.; the word māgaṇḍika (note ṇḍ) is not recorded in
PTSD or Childers; it seems doubtful whether it is related
to the n. pr. Māgandika (1).

<div n="lb"/>SHĀSTRA¦, SHĀSTER, {%s.%} A term applied to scientific or philo-
<div n="lb"/>sophical treatises and books of law. There are properly
<div n="lb"/>only six Ṣāstras, comprising the five philosophical sys-
<div n="lb"/>tems ({%see%} SCHOOL), and the code of legal institutes; viz.
<div n="lb"/>1. {#मीमांसाशास्त्रं#} or {#पूर्व्वमीमांसाशास्त्रं,#} 2. {#सांख्यशास्त्रं,#} 3. {#वेदान्तशास्त्रं,#}
<div n="lb"/>4. {#न्यायशास्त्रं,#} including under it the {#वैशेषिकशास्त्रं,#} 5. {#योगशास्त्रं,#}
<div n="lb"/>6. {#धर्म्मशास्त्रं#}. The last includes the celebrated code of
<div n="lb"/>Manu or {#मानवधर्म्मशास्त्रं#}. The term Ṣāstra is also applied
<div n="lb"/>to less important branches of literature, and when used in
<div n="lb"/>the singular number denotes the body of all that has
<div n="lb"/>been written on each subject; as, ‘Treatises on rhetoric,’
<div n="lb"/>{#अलङ्कारशास्त्रं;#} ‘poetical works,’ {#काव्यशास्त्रं;#} ‘Treatises on
<div n="lb"/>the mechanical arts,’ {#शिल्पिशास्त्रं#}.

TO¦, {%prep.%} Many of the meanings of ‘to’ are expressed by
<div n="lb"/>prepositions or affixes, or by the infin. mood, or are in-
<div n="lb"/>herent in the inflections of the Sanskrit noun; thus, (Mo-
<div n="lb"/>tion to or toward any place) expressed by the acc. case
<div n="lb"/>alone, or with {#प्रति#} &c. added; as, ‘he went to the city,’
<div n="lb"/>{#नगरं#} or {#नगरं प्रति जगाम,#} or by the gen. c., with {#अन्तिकं#} &c.;
<div n="lb"/>as, ‘he sent me to you,’ {#मां तवान्तिकं#} or {#तव सकाशम् अप्रैबीत्#}.
<div n="lb"/> — (Motion to any state) by the acc. c.; as, ‘he attains
<div n="lb"/>to fame,’ {#ख्यातिं याति#}. — (Adaptation) by the dat. c. or crude
<div n="lb"/>form; as, ‘that husband was not to her taste,’ {#स पतिस्#}
<div n="lb"/>{#तस्याः सन्तोषाय नाभवत्;#} ‘suited to royalty,’ {#राजयोग्यः -ग्या#}
<div n="lb"/>{#-ग्यं, राजार्हः#} &c.; ‘that is pleasing to me,’ {#तन् मह्यं रोचते#}. — 
<div n="lb"/>(Address, declaration, compellation) by the acc. c. or dat.
<div n="lb"/>c.; as, ‘he addressed a speech to the king,’ {#राजानं वचनम्#}
<div n="lb"/>{#अब्रवीत्;#} ‘speak to me,’ {#मां ब्रूहि;#} ‘I will declare this to
<div n="lb"/>my pupils,’ {#शिष्येभ्यः प्रवक्ष्यामि तत्;#} ‘he made it known to
<div n="lb"/>the king,’ {#तद् राज्ञे विज्ञापयति#}. — (Giving) by the dat., gen.,
<div n="lb"/>or loc. c.; ‘he gives sweetmeats to his son,’ {#पुत्राय#} or {#पुत्रस्य#}
<div n="lb"/>{#मोदकान् ददाति#}. — (Up to, as far as, denoting extent, degree,
<div n="lb"/>&c.) {#आ#} prefixed to the abl. or acc. c., {#पर्य्यन्त#} or {#अन्त#} in
<div n="lb"/>comp.; as, ‘to the ocean,’ {#आसमुद्रात्#} or {#समुद्रपर्य्यन्तं;#} ‘to the
<div n="lb"/>throat,’ {#आकण्ठात्;#} ‘to the knee,’ {#आजानु;#} ‘the numbers
<div n="lb"/>from one up to ten thousand,’ {#एकाद्ययुतान्तसंख्याः#}. — (Posses-
<div n="lb"/>sion, appertainment) by the gen. c.; as, ‘money belongs
<div n="lb"/>to me,’ {#मम धनं भवति#}. — (Purpose, intention, object) by the
<div n="lb"/>dat. c. or infin. mood, or by {#अर्थं#} &c. affixed; as, ‘the
<div n="lb"/>[Page806-a+ 51]
<div n="lb"/>sat down to consult,’ {#मन्त्रणाय#} or {#मन्त्रयितुं#} or {#मन्त्रणार्थम् उपविष्टाः;#}
<div n="lb"/>‘the barber came to shave his master,’ {#भर्त्तृक्षौरकरणाय नापित#}

<div n="lb"/>(As a sign of the infinitive) expressed by the infinitive,

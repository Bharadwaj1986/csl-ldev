<div n="lb"/>LEST¦, {%conj.%} {#यदि, यदि न, चेत्, न चेत्, नो चेत्, यदि कदाचित्, यदा-#}
<div n="lb"/>{#कदाचित्, कदाचित्#}. In using these particles to express
<div n="lb"/>the word ‘lest’ it will be necessary to vary the form
<div n="lb"/>of the sentence according to the particle employed, as
<div n="lb"/>in the following examples selected from modern Sanskrit.
<div n="lb"/>‘I fear lest your minds should be corrupted,’ {#चेद् युष्माकं#}
<div n="lb"/>{#मनांसि दुष्येयुर् ममैषा भीतिर् जायते;#} ‘beware lest he strike
<div n="lb"/>thee,’ {#सावधानो भव चेत् त्वामपि ताडयति;#} or the particle may
<div n="lb"/>be omitted; as, ‘beware lest any man deceive you,’
<div n="lb"/>{#सावधाना भवत कोऽपि युप्मान् न भ्रमयेत्:#} or other particles may
<div n="lb"/>be used in more classical Sanskrit, the construction of
<div n="lb"/>the sentence being changed accordingly; as, ‘commit
<div n="lb"/>not evil lest thou obtain the same,’ {#पापं मा कुरु पापे हि कृते#}
<div n="lb"/>{#त्वया तादृशं प्राप्तव्यं;#} ‘he commanded to kill the captives lest
<div n="lb"/>they should escape,’ {#वन्दयः पलायिष्यन्ते इत्याशङ्कया#} or {#इति बुद्ध्या#}
<div n="lb"/>{#तान् हन्तुम् आदिदेश;#} ‘justice is not to be killed, lest being
<div n="lb"/>killed, it destroy us,’ {#धर्म्मो न हन्तव्यो मा नो धर्म्मो हतो बधीत्#}.

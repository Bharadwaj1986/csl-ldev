<div n="lb"/>{#अरण्यमध्ये#}. When ‘into’ follows verbs of motion it may
<div n="lb"/>be expressed by the accus. c.; as, ‘come into the house,’
<div n="lb"/>{#गृहम् आयाहि;#} ‘the river runs into the ocean,’ {#नदी समुद्रं#}
<div n="lb"/>{#विशति#}. When it follows verbs denoting examination or
<div n="lb"/>inspection it may also be expressed by the acc. c.; as,
<div n="lb"/>‘he looks into the letter,’ {#पत्रं निरूपयति#}. When it follows
<div n="lb"/>verbs denoting insertion or inclusion it may be expressed
<div n="lb"/>by the loc. c.; as, ‘he inserts the wedge into the wood,’
<div n="lb"/>{#कीलं काष्ठे निवेशयति#}. When it follows verbs denoting
<div n="lb"/>mixture it may be expressed by the instrum. c., or by
<div n="lb"/>{#सह#} with the instrum. c.; as, ‘he puts the wine into the
<div n="lb"/>water,’ {#मद्यं जलेन सह मिश्रयति#}. When ‘into’ denotes the
<div n="lb"/>passing of a thing from one state to another it may be
<div n="lb"/>expressed by the affixed {#सात्;#} as, ‘converted into water,’
<div n="lb"/>{#जलसास्कृतः -ता -तं,#} or by changing the final of the noun
<div n="lb"/>[Page408-a+ 51]
<div n="lb"/>to {#ई;#} as, ‘reduced into one mass,’ {#पिण्डीभूतः -ता -तं#}.
<div n="lb"/>When ‘into’ is used for ‘in’ it may usually be expressed
<div n="lb"/>by the loc. c.; as, ‘into the ear,’ {#कर्णे#}. This last expres-
<div n="lb"/>sion is also rendered by {#उपकर्णं#}.

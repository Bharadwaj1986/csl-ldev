<div n="lb"/>IN¦, {%prep.%} {#नि#} prefixed to roots may give the sense of ‘in;’ as,
<div n="lb"/>‘to enter in,’ {#निविश्;#} ‘to dwell in,’ {#निवस्#}. When ‘in’
<div n="lb"/>is used for ‘within,’ or ‘in the midst,’ it may be expressed
<div n="lb"/>by {#अन्तर्, अन्तरा, अन्तरे, मध्ये#}. But the most frequent use
<div n="lb"/>of ‘in’ is expressed in Sanskrit by the locative case,
<div n="lb"/>without any preposition; as, ‘in the city,’ {#नगरे;#} ‘in the
<div n="lb"/>night,’ {#रात्रौ;#} ‘a tree planted in the earth,’ {#पृथिव्यां रोपितो#}
<div n="lb"/>{#वृक्षः;#} ‘confidence in you,’ {#त्वयि विश्वासः;#} ‘engaging in the
<div n="lb"/>acquisition of wealth,’ {#अर्थार्जने प्रवृत्तिः#} {%f.;%} ‘the sun in the
<div n="lb"/>east,’ {#प्राचि सूर्य्यः;#} ‘he sinks in the mud,’ {#पङ्के मज्जति;#} ‘he
<div n="lb"/>delights in the good of others,’ {#परहिते रमते;#} ‘in the front
<div n="lb"/>of the fight,’ {#रणमूर्द्धि#}. In cases where ‘in’ refers to any
<div n="lb"/>division of time, or the manner in which any thing is
<div n="lb"/>done, it is usually expressed in Sanskrit by the instr.
<div n="lb"/>case; as, ‘in three years,’ {#त्रिभिर् वर्षैः;#} ‘in twelve months,’
<div n="lb"/>{#द्वादशभिर् मासैः;#} ‘in hundreds of years,’ {#वर्षशतैः;#} ‘in an
<div n="lb"/>instant,’ {#क्षणेन;#} ‘in process of time,’ {#कालपर्य्यायेण;#} ‘in
<div n="lb"/>how long time?’ {#कियता कालेन;#} ‘in a short time,’ {#अचिरेण;#}
<div n="lb"/>‘in this manner,’ {#अनेन प्रकारेण;#} ‘in abundance,’ {#बाहुल्येन;#}
<div n="lb"/>‘in the form of a man,’ {#नररूपेण;#} ‘in joke,’ {#परिहासेन;#}
<div n="lb"/>‘written in Sanskrit,’ {#संस्कृतेन रचितं#}. Certain adverbial
<div n="lb"/>affixes and prefixes may often express ‘in;’ as, ‘in front,’
<div n="lb"/>{#अग्रतस्;#} ‘in the first place,’ {#प्रथमतस्;#} ‘in another place,’
<div n="lb"/>{#अन्यत्र;#} ‘in one place,’ {#एकत्र;#} ‘in many places,’ {#बहुत्र;#} ‘in
<div n="lb"/>the next world,’ {#अमुत्र;#} ‘in every way,’ {#सर्व्वथा;#} ‘in ano-
<div n="lb"/>ther way,’ {#अन्यथा;#} ‘in one way,’ {#एकधा;#} ‘in a hundred
<div n="lb"/>ways,’ {#शतधा;#} ‘in many ways,’ {#बहुधा;#} ‘in some way,’
<div n="lb"/>[Page371-a+ 51]
<div n="lb"/>{#कथञ्चित्, कथञ्चन;#} ‘in small quantities,’ {#अल्पशस्;#} ‘in large
<div n="lb"/>quantities,’ {#बहुशस्;#} ‘in proper order,’ {#क्रमशस्, क्रमतस्,#}
<div n="lb"/>{#अनुक्रमं;#} ‘in every direction,’ {#प्रतिदेशं;#} ‘in three direc-
<div n="lb"/>tions,’ {#त्रेधा;#} ‘in like manner,’ {#तथैव, तद्वत्;#} ‘in vain,’
<div n="lb"/>{#वृथा, मुधा#}. Sometimes ‘in’ may be expressed by a par-
<div n="lb"/>ticiple; as, ‘he has pleasure in calumniating others,’
<div n="lb"/>{#अन्यान् परिवदन् स तुप्यति#} or {#पराभिशंसनं कृत्वा तुष्टो भवति;#} ‘what
<div n="lb"/>bravery is there in killing a sleeping man?’ {#किं पौरुषं हत्वा#}
<div n="lb"/>{#सुप्तं;#} ‘he said that to me in joke,’ {#उपहस्य माम् तद् अवदत्#}.
<div n="lb"/>The sense of ‘in,’ may be inherent in a noun; as, ‘to
<div n="lb"/>go in and out,’ {#गमनागमने कृ#}. ‘In as much as,’ is ex-
<div n="lb"/>pressed by {#यस्मात्, येन,#} or by the abl. or instr. case of the
<div n="lb"/>abstract noun; as, ‘in as much as it is founded on
<div n="lb"/>observation,’ {#प्रत्यक्षमूलत्वात्#} or {#प्रत्यक्षमूलतया#}.

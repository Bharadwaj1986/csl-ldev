<div n="lb"/>THAN¦, {%adv.%} or {%conj.%} Usually expressed in Sanskrit by the
<div n="lb"/>abl. c.; as, ‘a store of grain is better than all stores,’ {#धान्य-#}
<div n="lb"/>{#संग्रहः श्रेयान् सर्व्वसंग्रहात्;#} ‘worthier than I,’ {#अस्मत्तो वरीयान्#}.
<div n="lb"/>Or by {#न च;#} as, ‘silence is better than speaking un-
<div n="lb"/>truth,’ {#वरं मौनं न च वचनम् अनृतं#} [{%i. e.%} better silence and
<div n="lb"/>not speaking untruth]. In modern Sanskrit ‘than’ is
<div n="lb"/>often expressed by {#अपेक्षया,#} or {#अपेक्ष्य;#} as, ‘some of the
<div n="lb"/>organs of animals are stronger than those of men,’ {#जन्तूनां#}
<div n="lb"/>{#कानिचिद् इन्द्रियाणि मनुप्यापेक्षया प्रवलानि;#} ‘acid has a stronger
<div n="lb"/>attraction for iron than copper,’ {#अम्लरसं तास्रम् अपेक्ष्य लोहम्#}
<div n="lb"/>{#अधिकम् आकर्षति;#} ‘placing the cup on some place higher
<div n="lb"/>than one's head,’ {#स्वमस्तकम् अपेक्ष्य उच्चतरे स्थाने पात्रं विन्यस्य;#}
<div n="lb"/>‘than him,’ {#तदपेक्षया;#} ‘than me,’ {#मदपेक्षया#}.

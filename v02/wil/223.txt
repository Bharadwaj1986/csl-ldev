{#अगस्ति#}¦ m. ({#-स्तिः#})
.²1 The name of a saint, celebrated in Hindu mythology, more usually entitled
AGASTYA, the son of both MITRA and VARUṆA, by URVAŚĪ; he is represented of short
stature, and is said by some to have been born in a water jar: he is famed for
having swallowed the ocean, when it had given him offence; at his command also,
the VINDHYA range of mountains prostrated itself, and so remains; hence his
present appellation: he is also considered as the regent of the star Canopus.
.²2 The name of a tree, (<bot>Sesbana grandiflora</bot>.)
.E. {#अग#} a mountain, and {#स्ति#}, with an adventitious meaning, to fix, or in
the second instance, to be fixed; also {#अग#} as before, and {#स्ति#} Uṇādi
aff.

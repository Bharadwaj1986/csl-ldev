<L>39795<pc>876<k1>संस्कार<k2>संस्कार
{#संस्कार#}¦ m. ({#-रः#})
.²1 Completing, accomplishing, finishing, perfecting.
.²2 Apprehension, conception.
.²3 The power of memory, the faculty of recollection.
.²4 Faculty in general, one of the twenty-four qualities enumerated by
logicians.
.²5 An essential 
[Page877]
 and purificatory rite or ceremony amongst the
{%Hindus;%} as those of the three first classes, commencing with conception and
ending with marriage; or {#गर्भाघानं#} sacrifice on conception;
{#पुंसवनं#} on vitality in the fœtus; {#सीमन्तोन्नयनं#} in the fourth,
sixth, or eighth month; {#जातकर्म्म#} giving the infant clarified butter out of
a golden spoon to taste, before dividing the navel string; {#नामकरणं#}
naming the child on the tenth, eleventh, twelfth, or hundred and first day:
{#निःक्रमणं#} carrying him out to see the moon on the third lunar day of the
third light fortnight, or to see the sun in the third or fourth month;
{#अन्नप्राशनं#} feeding him with rice in the sixth or eighth month, or when he
has cut teeth: {#चूडाकार्य्यं#} tonsure in the second or third year:
{#उपनयनं#} investiture with the string in the fifth, eighth, or sixteenth
year: {#विवाहः#} or marriage is the tenth and last {%Saṃskāra.%}
.²5 Embellishment, decoration, elegance.
.²6 Purity, perfection.
.²7 Preparing as an article of medicine or food, cooking, dressing, compounding,
&c.
.²8 Purification, consecration.
.²9 Impression, form, mould.
.E. {#सम्#} implying perfection, {#कृ#} to make, {#अण्#} aff. and {#सुट्#}
augment.

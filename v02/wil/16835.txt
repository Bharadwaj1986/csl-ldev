.²1 To conduct, to drive or guide, to cause progressive conveyance.
.²2 To obtain, to get. The root is inflected as the deponent verb, implying.
.²1 {%Instruction,%} as {#नयते शास्त्रे#} he instructs in the {%Śāstra;%}
.²2 {%Worshipping,%} {#विष्णुंनयते#} he worships VIṢṆU; also
with prepositions in the sense of; 
.²1 {%Paying,%} {#भृत्यमुपनयते#} he pays the hire; 
.²2 {%Paying as a debt,%} {#ऋणम्विनयते#} he discharges the debt; 
.²3 {%Casting or lifting up,%} {#दण्डमुन्नयते#} he lifts up the stick; 
.²4 {%Giving,%} {#द्रव्यम्बिनयते#} he gives the things; and 
.²5 {%Brahmanical Investiture,%} {#पुत्त्रमुपनयते#} he invests his son with the thread: it is also deponent, governing an incorporeal object existing in the agent, as {#क्रोधम्विनयते#} he suppresses wrath; if the object is not in the agent the verb is active, as {#गुरोःक्रोधम्विनयति#} he appeases his teacher's anger; as also if the object is corporal, as {#गण्डं विनयति#} he turns away the cheek.
